**Q: What is the typical harvest season, and how does it affect availability?**
A: Northern Hemisphere harvest runs August–October. Crop quality reports are available from July onward. We contract 80% of annual volume pre-harvest to secure pricing. Spot availability is lowest March–May.

**Q: How is raw material quality verified at receiving?**
A: Every inbound lot is sampled and tested for moisture (≤8.0%, AOAC 925.10), protein (18–24%, AOAC 984.13), fat (38–44%, AOAC 920.39), foreign material (≤0.5%), and visual inspection for mold, insects, and off-odours. Reject criteria: moisture >10%, visible mold, or live insect infestation.

## Products & Specifications

**Q: What are the five flaxseed fractions, and what distinguishes them?**
A: FlaxseedPro offers five structurally-defined fractions: **Hull** (outer seed coat, TDF 70–80%, SDG lignans 7–10 mg/g), **Kernel** (dehulled seed, protein 30–35%, fat 40–45%), **Powder** (milled kernel or whole seed, full-fat or defatted, 20–100 mesh), **Gum** (aqueous-extracted polysaccharide, viscosity 500–5000 cP at 1%), and **Oil** (cold-pressed or refined, ALA 55–60%). Each fraction has a dedicated technical specification document with complete compositional, physical, and microbiological data.

**Q: What is the difference between flaxseed hull and kernel?**
A: The hull is the fibrous outer layer (30–35% of seed mass) containing 70–80% dietary fiber and 7–10 mg/g SDG lignans. The kernel is the lipid- and protein-rich inner fraction (60–65% of seed mass) containing 40–45% fat and 30–35% protein. They are separated by mechanical dehulling and air classification.

**Q: What is flaxseed gum, and what are its primary applications?**
A: Flaxseed gum (mucilage) is a heteropolysaccharide complex extracted from the hull. It is composed of 7 monosaccharides (xylose 30–38%, galactose 20–28%, rhamnose 12–18%), exhibits pseudoplastic rheology (n = 0.3–0.5), and functions as a clean-label emulsifier, thickener, and water binder. Primary applications: gluten-free baking, plant-based beverages, salad dressings, and meat analogue binders.

**Q: What grades of flaxseed oil do you offer?**
A: Two grades: **Cold-Pressed** (FSP-OIL-001) — mechanically extracted below 40°C, FFA ≤0.5%, PV ≤2.0 meq/kg, smoke point 107°C; and **Physically Refined** (FSP-OIL-002) — deodorized for neutral flavour, FFA ≤0.1%, PV ≤1.0 meq/kg, smoke point 225°C. Both grades contain ALA 55–60% and tocopherols 800–1200 mg/kg.

**Q: What is the difference between full-fat and defatted flaxseed powder?**
A: Full-fat powder (FSP-PDR-001) retains native oil (35–42% fat, protein 20–25%) and is suited for bakery applications where fat contributes to texture. Defatted powder (FSP-PDR-002) has oil removed by pressing (6–12% fat, protein 40–50%) and is preferred for protein enrichment where higher protein concentration and longer shelf life (12 vs 6 months ambient) are required.

## Processing

**Q: What is the difference between cold-pressed and solvent-extracted flaxseed oil?**
A: Cold pressing uses mechanical screw pressing below 40°C, yielding 32–38% oil with full flavour and antioxidant retention. Solvent extraction (hexane, 55–60°C) achieves >99% oil recovery from press cake, producing defatted meal (<1.5% residual oil). The solvent-extracted oil is refined and blended with cold-pressed oil or sold separately for non-food applications.

**Q: What is cryogenic grinding, and when would I need it?**
A: Cryogenic grinding uses liquid nitrogen (−150°C) to embrittle the seed before milling. It limits product temperature rise to <10°C (vs. 15–25°C for ambient hammer milling), preserving volatile aroma compounds and preventing lipid oxidation. Peroxide values are <1.0 meq/kg (vs. ≤2.0 for ambient). Recommended for high-ALA full-fat powders where oxidative stability is critical.

**Q: Can you customise particle size for powder products?**
A: Yes. Six standard mesh grades are available: 20, 30, 40, 60, 80, and 100 mesh. Custom specifications within ±1 mesh tolerance require minimum 5 MT per order. Particle size distribution (D10, D50, D90 by laser diffraction) is provided on the COA.

**Q: What is the dehulling efficiency, and how is it controlled?**
A: Our centrifugal impact dehuller achieves 85–90% dehulling efficiency at 3 MT/hr. The hull and kernel fractions are separated by zigzag air classification (terminal velocity differential: hull 1–2 m/s, kernel 4–6 m/s) followed by gravity separation. Kernel purity is ≥95%, hull purity ≥92%, checked every 2 hours.

## Technical Applications

**Q: What are the recommended usage levels for each flaxseed fraction?**
A: General guidelines: **Hull**: 2–10% in bakery for fibre enrichment (adjust water +6–8× hull weight). **Kernel**: 5–20% in bakery or as oil press feedstock. **Powder** (full-fat): 5–15% in bakery, 10–25% in protein blends. **Powder** (defatted): 10–30% in protein applications. **Gum**: 0.2–2% as thickener or emulsifier. **Oil**: 2–10% in dressings or supplements. Detailed formulation guides are available for each product.

**Q: How does flaxseed hull affect water absorption in baking?**
A: Flaxseed hull has a water holding capacity of 6–8 g/g (vs. wheat flour at approximately 0.6–0.8 g/g). When substituting flour with hull, water must be increased by 6–8× the hull weight to maintain dough consistency. For example, replacing 100 g of flour with hull requires an additional 600–800 mL of water. We recommend pre-hydrating the hull for 15–20 minutes before incorporation.

**Q: Can your products be used in plant-based meat alternatives?**
A: Yes. Defatted flaxseed powder (40–50% protein) functions as a binder and protein contributor. Flaxseed gum (0.5–1.5%) improves water binding and texture in plant-based burgers and sausages. Whole flaxseed meal contributes both fibre and fat for juiciness. Extruded flaxseed protein concentrate is under development.

**Q: What is the recommended storage for each product?**
A: **Whole seed/kernel**: <25°C, <60% RH, 12 months. **Full-fat powder**: 6 months ambient, 9 months refrigerated (N₂ flushed). **Defatted powder**: 12 months ambient. **Hull**: 12 months. **Gum**: 24 months (<50% RH, hygroscopic). **Oil** (cold-pressed): 18 months unopened, 60 days after opening (refrigerate). **Oil** (refined): 24 months unopened.

**Q: Can flaxseed fractions be used in EU health claim applications?**
A: The EU has an authorised health claim for ALA: "ALA contributes to the maintenance of normal blood cholesterol levels" — condition of use: minimum 2 g ALA per day, stated as "high in omega-3 ALA" only if ≥0.6 g ALA per 100 g. Flaxseed oil at typical intake levels of 10–15 g/day delivers 5.5–9 g ALA, exceeding the claim threshold. No authorised claims currently exist for SDG lignans or flaxseed fibre. We recommend consulting regulatory counsel for specific claim validation.

**Q: Do your products contain allergens?**
A: Flaxseed is not listed among the major allergens in the US (FALCPA + FASTER: 9 major + sesame), EU (14 regulated allergens), or China, but individual sensitivity has been reported. Our facility handles ONLY flaxseed — no dairy, soy, eggs, peanuts, tree nuts, wheat, fish, or shellfish are present. All products are tested gluten-free (GFCO certified, <10 ppm). Cross-contact risk is managed through dedicated equipment and validated cleaning.
