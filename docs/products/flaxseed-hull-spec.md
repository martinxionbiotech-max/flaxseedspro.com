## 1. Product Description

Flaxseed Hull is the outer seed coat (testa) of *Linum usitatissimum* L., mechanically separated from the kernel through a proprietary dry-dehulling process. The hull fraction is enriched in insoluble and soluble dietary fibers, lignans (primarily secoisolariciresinol diglucoside, SDG), and associated polyphenolic compounds. It is intended for use as a functional food ingredient, dietary fiber supplement base, and bioactive compound concentrate for nutraceutical formulations. The product is milled to a controlled particle size distribution and is free from foreign matter, visible mold, and insect infestation.

The dehulling process employs a multi-stage impact dehuller followed by aspiration and sieving trains (three-pass separation) to achieve a minimum 92 % hull purity with less than 8 % residual kernel (endosperm + cotyledon) as determined by microscopic imaging analysis (ISO 13322-2). The final milling step uses a pin mill operating at 3,600 RPM with a 0.8 mm screen aperture.

## 2. Proximate Composition

Analytical parameters determined on an as-is basis. Results are reported as the mean of duplicate analyses.

| **Parameter**           | **Specification** | **Typical Value** | **Method**      |
| ----------------------- | ----------------- | ----------------- | --------------- |
| Total Dietary Fiber     | 70 – 80 %         | 74.3 %            | AOAC 991.43     |
| Soluble Dietary Fiber   | 25 – 35 %         | 28.1 %            | AOAC 991.43     |
| Insoluble Dietary Fiber | 40 – 48 %         | 46.2 %            | AOAC 991.43     |
| Protein (N × 6.25)      | 10 – 15 %         | 12.8 %            | AOAC 984.13     |
| Fat (Crude, Acid Hydrolysis) | 8 – 12 %     | 10.4 %            | AOAC 920.39     |
| Moisture                | ≤ 6.0 %           | 4.2 %             | AOAC 925.10     |
| Ash                     | 3 – 5 %           | 4.1 %             | AOAC 923.03     |
| Carbohydrates (by difference) | 3 – 5 %      | 4.1 %             | Calculation     |

**Notes:**
- AOAC 991.43 (Total, Soluble, and Insoluble Dietary Fiber) uses the enzymatic–gravimetric method with phosphate buffer and Termamyl®, protease, and amyloglucosidase digestion.
- AOAC 984.13 (Protein) employs the Kjeldahl digestion method with copper catalyst; conversion factor N × 6.25.
- AOAC 920.39 (Crude Fat) uses the Soxhlet extraction method with petroleum ether (40–60 °C boiling range), 6-hour extraction cycle.
- AOAC 925.10 (Moisture) is determined by forced-air oven drying at 105 °C for 3 hours.
- AOAC 923.03 (Ash) by muffle furnace incineration at 550 °C for 6 hours.
- Carbohydrates calculated by difference: 100 – (moisture + protein + fat + ash + TDF). Note that TDF is a subset of total carbohydrate so the "by difference" value represents primarily low-molecular-weight sugars and organic acids; typical sucrose content by HPLC (AOAC 2013.12) is < 0.5 %.

**Particle Size Measurement Details:**
- Instrument: Malvern Mastersizer 3000 with Aero S dry dispersion unit.
- Dispersion air pressure: 1.5 bar. Feed rate: 40 %. Refractive index: 1.53 (flaxseed hull), absorption index: 0.01.
- Measurement range: 0.01 – 3,500 µm. Obscuration: 0.5 – 5 %. Results averaged over 5 consecutive measurements.
- Span calculation: (D90 − D10) / D50.

**Hydration Property Methods:**
- WHC: 1 g sample + 20 mL deionized water, stirred for 5 min, held at 20 °C for 1 h, centrifuged at 3,000 × g for 20 min, supernatant decanted, pellet weighed. WHC = (pellet weight − sample weight) / sample weight.
- OHC: 1 g sample + 10 mL sunflower oil (density 0.918 g/mL), vortexed 1 min, held 30 min at 20 °C, centrifuged at 3,000 × g for 20 min, oil decanted, pellet weighed. OHC = (pellet weight − sample weight) / sample weight.
- Swelling: 1 g sample in 10 mL water in a graduated cylinder, dispersed, held 24 h at 25 °C. Swelling = (final sediment volume − initial volume) / initial volume (mL/g).

- TDF retention at 12 months: > 98 % of initial value.
- SDG retention at 12 months: > 95 % of initial value (HPLC-UV).
- ORAC retention at 12 months: > 92 % of initial value.
