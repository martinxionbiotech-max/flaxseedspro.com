**Document ID:** PROD-PROC-003

**Product:** Flaxseed Oil (Cold-Pressed & Solvent-Extracted)

## 1. Process Overview

Flaxseed oil is produced via two primary routes: **Cold Press** (premium, retains natural properties) and **Solvent Extraction** (maximum yield, lower grade). Cold-pressed oil may optionally undergo **refining** (degumming, neutralization, bleaching, deodorization) for specific customer requirements.

### Process Flow Diagrams

#### Cold Press Flow

```
Flaxseed
    ↓
Cleaning & Drying
    ↓
  Screw Press (CCP-2)
    ↓
Crude Oil ──→ Press Cake
    ↓              ↓
  Filter         Defatted Meal
    ↓          (to powder line)
  Crude Oil Tank
    ↓
[Optional Refining]
┌───┴───┐
↓       ↓
Refined  Bottling
Oil      (Virgin)
```

#### Solvent Extraction Flow

```
Flaxseed (or Press Cake)
        ↓
  Flaking / Conditioning
        ↓
  Solvent Extractor (Hexane)
        ↓
   ┌────┴────┐
   ↓         ↓
Miscella    Spent Meal
   ↓           ↓
Evaporator   Desolventizer
   ↓           ↓
Crude Oil   Defatted Meal
   ↓        (to powder line)
Refining (full wet process)
```

## 2. Detailed Process Steps — Cold Press

### 2.1 Seed Cleaning & Drying

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Seed moisture target | 6.0 – 8.0% |
| Foreign matter | ≤0.5% |
| Cleaning equipment | Scalper + aspirator + destoner |
| Magnetic separator | Fe ≥2.0 mm removal |
| Drying temperature (if needed) | ≤45°C (fluid bed dryer) |

- **Quality Gate:** Moisture content recorded per batch. If >8.5%, dry before pressing.
- **Note:** Over-drying (<6%) reduces press throughput and increases oil oxidation.

### 2.2 Screw Press — CCP-2

The screw press (expeller) mechanically extracts oil from flaxseed. Temperature control is critical to preserve omega-3 fatty acids.

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Screw speed | 15 RPM (typical range: 10–20 RPM) |
| Barrel temperature | <40°C (cold press) |
| Nozzle (choke) diameter | 8 mm (range: 6–12 mm) |
| Motor power | 15 – 30 kW |
| Throughput | 50 – 150 kg seed/hr per press |
| Oil yield | 70–85% of total oil (cold press) |
| Residual oil in cake | 12–18% |

#### Temperature Management

- Barrel temperature is the **critical parameter**. Monitor with thermocouple embedded in the press barrel.
- **CCP-2 Critical Limit:** Barrel temperature must not exceed **40°C** for cold-pressed grade.
- If barrel temperature approaches 38°C:
  1. Reduce feed rate by 10%.
  2. Check cooling jacket water flow (if equipped).
  3. Verify screw speed is at 15 RPM.
- Cooling jackets (water or glycol) should maintain barrel at 30–38°C.

#### Press Setup Procedure

1. Pre-heat press to 30°C by running seed at low feed rate for 10 min.
2. Set screw speed to 15 RPM.
3. Set nozzle gap to 8 mm.
4. Start seed feed at 50% of target rate; ramp to full rate over 5 min.
5. Monitor barrel temperature; stabilize before adjusting feed.

- **Quality Gate:** Barrel temperature ≤40°C recorded every 15 min in CCP log.

### 2.3 Crude Oil Filtration

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Equipment | Plate-and-frame filter press |
| Filter media | Polypropylene filter cloth (10–20 µm) |
| Filtration pressure | ≤4 bar |
| Temperature | 30–40°C |
| Fines in filtered oil | ≤0.1% w/w |

- **Pre-coat:** Diatomaceous earth (optional, for polishing).
- **Quality Gate:** Visual clarity — clear, no visible sediment.

### 2.4 Crude Oil Storage (Before Refining or Bottling)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Tank material | SS304 or SS316 |
| Temperature | 15–20°C (cooled storage) |
| Headspace | Nitrogen blanket (O₂ ≤2.0%) |
| Light protection | Opaque tank or covered |
| Maximum storage duration | 7 days (crude) / 30 days (refined) |

## 3. Detailed Process Steps — Solvent Extraction

### 3.1 Flaking & Conditioning

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Flake thickness | 0.2 – 0.4 mm |
| Flake moisture | 8 – 10% |
| Conditioning temperature | 55 – 65°C |
| Conditioning time | 20 – 30 minutes |

- **Purpose:** Rupture cell walls for efficient hexane penetration.

### 3.2 Solvent Extraction

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Solvent | Hexane (food-grade, boiling range 65–70°C) |
| Extractor type | Rotocel or shallow-bed (De Smet / Crown) |
| Solvent-to-feed ratio | 1:1 to 1.5:1 (w/w) |
| Extraction temperature | 50 – 60°C |
| Extraction time | 30 – 60 min (total cycle) |
| Miscella concentration | 20 – 30% oil in hexane |

- **Quality Gate:** Residual oil in spent meal ≤1.0% (checked per shift).

### 3.3 Miscella Evaporation

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| First stage evaporator | 70 – 80°C, atmospheric |
| Second stage evaporator | 90 – 100°C, vacuum (−0.6 bar) |
| Stripper column | 100 – 110°C, steam injection |
| Residual hexane in oil | ≤500 ppm |
| Recovered hexane | Recycled to extractor |

### 3.4 Desolventizing (Spent Meal)

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Desolventizer type | DTDC (Desolventizer-Toaster-Dryer-Cooler) |
| Temperature | 100 – 110°C (indirect steam) |
| Residual hexane in meal | ≤500 ppm |
| Final meal moisture | ≤10% |

- **Output:** Defatted meal → to Flaxseed Powder (Defatted) line.

## 4. Oil Refining (Optional — Both Cold Press & Solvent Extracted)

Refining is applied when a neutral, light-colored, bland oil is required. Each step is optional depending on target specification.

### 4.1 Degumming

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Temperature | 60 – 70°C |
| Water addition | 2 – 3% of oil weight (hot water) |
| Mixing time | 20 – 30 min, high-shear |
| Centrifuge | Disc stack, 7,000 – 9,000 RPM |
| Phosphorus after degumming | ≤20 ppm (from 200–600 ppm crude) |
| Hydratable gums removed | Yes (lecithin by-product) |

- **Quality Gate:** Phosphorus content ≤20 ppm.

### 4.2 Neutralization (Alkali Refining)

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Temperature | 75 – 85°C |
| Alkali | NaOH solution, 16–20°Bé |
| Excess alkali | 0.1 – 0.3% above stoichiometric |
| Contact time | 15 – 20 min (high-speed mixer) |
| Centrifuge separation | Disc stack, 8,000 – 10,000 RPM |
| Water wash | 10 – 15% hot water (90–95°C), 1–2 washes |
| Final FFA | ≤0.05% (from 0.5–2.0% crude) |
| Soap content after wash | ≤50 ppm |

- **Quality Gate:** FFA ≤0.05% after neutralization.

### 4.3 Bleaching

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Temperature | 90 – 105°C |
| Vacuum | −0.08 to −0.09 MPa |
| Bleaching earth (Tonsil / Bentonite) | 0.5 – 2.0% w/w |
| Contact time | 20 – 30 min |
| Filtration | Vertical leaf filter or plate & frame |
| Color reduction | Lovibond 5.25" cell: from 30–70Y + 3–7R to ≤10Y + 1.0R |

- **Quality Gate:** Bleached oil color ≤10Y + 1.0R (Lovibond, 5.25" cell).
- **Note:** Activated carbon (0.1–0.3%) may be added for stubborn color bodies.

### 4.4 Deodorization

| Parameter | Value / Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Temperature | 200 – 230°C |
| Vacuum | −0.09 to −0.095 MPa (3–5 mbar absolute) |
| Steam sparge rate | 1 – 3% of oil weight per hour |
| Time | 60 – 120 min |
| Cooling | Cool to ≤40°C before breaking vacuum |

- **Quality Gate:** PV ≤1.0 meq O₂/kg; FFA ≤0.05%; odor bland.
- **Cold test:** Must pass 24-hour cold test at 0°C (no crystallization → indicates no winterization needed for most applications).
- **Antioxidant addition:** Add 200 ppm TBHQ or 500 ppm rosemary extract during cool-down.

## 5. Quality Gates (In-Process Checks)

| Check | Frequency | Method | Acceptable Limit | Action if Out of Spec |
|-------|-----------|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| Barrel temperature (press) | Every 15 min | Thermocouple | ≤40°C | Reduce feed; check cooling |
| Crude oil PV | Per batch | AOCS Cd 8-53 | ≤2.0 meq O₂/kg | Flash to N₂; reduce storage temp |
| Crude oil FFA | Per batch | AOCS Ca 5a-40 | ≤1.5% | Refining can correct; log for refiner |
| Oil color (after bleaching) | Per batch | Lovibond (5.25") | ≤10Y + 1.0R | Increase earth dose or re-bleach |
| Oil color (final) | Per batch | Lovibond (5.25") | ≤5Y + 0.5R (refined) | Check bleaching step |
| Refined oil PV | Per batch | AOCS Cd 8-53 | ≤1.0 meq O₂/kg | Add antioxidant; check N₂ blanket |
| FFA (refined) | Per batch | AOCS Ca 5a-40 | ≤0.05% | Re-neutralize or blend |
| Cold test | Per batch | 0°C / 24 hr | No crystallization | Winterize or adjust pressing conditions |
| Residual hexane | Per batch | GC headspace | ≤500 ppm | Increase stripper temperature |
| Phosphorus (degummed) | Per batch | AOCS Ca 12-55 | ≤20 ppm | Repeat degumming |

## 6. CCP Integration — CCP-2 (Chemical — Process Contaminants)

| CCP Code | Hazard Type | Control Point | Critical Limit | Monitoring | Corrective Action |
|----------|-------------|---------------|----------------|------------|-------------------|
| CCP-2 | Chemical (oxidation / PAH / trans fat from overheating) | Screw press (barrel temperature) | Barrel temp ≤40°C (cold press) | Continuous thermocouple + manual check every 15 min | If temp >40°C: reduce feed rate, check cooling, hold oil for PV/FFA testing. If PV >2.0 meq/kg or FFA >1.5%, divert to refined oil stream. |

**Supporting Rationale:** Overheating during pressing promotes:
- Lipid oxidation (high PV)
- Free fatty acid formation (hydrolysis)
- Potential formation of processing contaminants (e.g., 3-MCPD, glycidyl esters at >200°C — relevant during deodorization, monitored separately)

**Deodorization CCP (if applicable):** Deodorization temperature ≥200°C ensures removal of volatile contaminants (pesticides, PAHs). Critical limit: 200–230°C for ≥60 min.

## 7. Troubleshooting

### 7.1 High Peroxide Value (PV > 2.0 meq/kg)

| Possible Cause | Check | Solution |
|---------------|-------|----------|
| Press barrel too hot | Barrel thermocouple | Reduce feed; verify cooling jacket function |
| Old / rancid seed | Raw material age / PV | Use seed ≤6 months post-harvest; cold storage |
| High storage temperature | Tank temp log | Reduce to ≤20°C; ensure N₂ blanket |
| Oxygen exposure | Headspace O₂ | Increase N₂ flow; minimize headspace |
| Metal contamination | Metal detector check | Check for wear metal from press |
| Light exposure | Storage vessel cover | Use opaque tanks or light-proof covers |

### 7.2 Dark Color (Oil Too Dark)

| Possible Cause | Check | Solution |
|---------------|-------|----------|
| Press temperature too high | Barrel temp log | Reduce to ≤40°C |
| Seed scorching | Press visual inspection | Clean press; reduce choke (larger nozzle) |
| High chlorophyll (immature seed) | Seed color spec | Source golden flaxseed (lower chlorophyll) |
| Iron contamination | Metal detector / magnet | Install magnet in seed feed line |
| Filtration inadequate | Filter press pressure | Replace filter cloth; add pre-coat |
| Over-bleaching (if refining) | Bleaching earth dosage | Reduce earth to ≤1.5%; check contact time |

### 7.3 Poor Oil Yield (Cold Press)

| Possible Cause | Check | Solution |
|---------------|-------|----------|
| Seed moisture too high (>8.5%) | Moisture analyzer | Dry seed to 6–8% |
| Seed moisture too low (<6%) | Moisture analyzer | Tempering — hold seed at 70% RH for 12 hr |
| Screw speed wrong | RPM reading | Adjust to 15 RPM |
| Nozzle size wrong | Nozzle diameter | Try 8 mm (smaller = higher pressure, more oil) |
| Worn press screw / barrel | Visual / feel | Replace worm shaft or barrel liners |
| Low oil content in seed | Seed spec sheet | Source seed with ≥38% oil content |

### 7.4 Emulsion Problems (Degumming / Washing)

| Possible Cause | Check | Solution |
|---------------|-------|----------|
| Water temperature too low | Thermometer | Use 90–95°C wash water |
| Insufficient mixing | Mixer RPM | Increase to ensure droplet dispersion |
| High gum content | Phosphorus in crude | Pre-degum with enzymatic degumming |
| Wrong centrifuge speed | Bowl RPM | Adjust to 8,000–10,000 RPM |
| pH imbalanced | pH of wash water | Maintain pH 6.5–7.0 |
| Over-neutralization | Excess NaOH | Reduce excess alkali to 0.1–0.15% |

### 7.5 Crystal Formation / Failed Cold Test

| Possible Cause | Check | Solution |
|---------------|-------|----------|
| High wax content | Wax content analysis | Winterize — cool to 4°C for 24 hr, filter |
| High saturated fats | Seed variety | Use low-saturate flax variety |
| Slow cooling rate | Cooling rate | Rapid cool to 0°C for winterization |
| Incomplete winterization | Filtration efficiency | Use 0.5 µm filter for wax removal |

### 7.6 High FFA in Crude Oil

| Possible Cause | Check | Solution |
|---------------|-------|----------|
| Damaged / sprouted seed | Seed inspection | Reject damaged seed lots |
| High moisture in storage | Silo moisture meter | Maintain ≤8% moisture during storage |
| Long storage before pressing | Seed age | Process within 3 months of harvest |
| Lipase enzyme activity | FFA trend over time | Flash heat seed to 90°C / 5 min to deactivate lipase |
| Press temperature high | Barrel temp log | Reduce to ≤40°C |

## 8. Line Comparison — Cold Press vs. Solvent Extraction

| Aspect | Cold Press | Solvent Extraction |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| Oil yield | 70–85% | 97–99% |
| Residual oil in meal | 12–18% | ≤1.0% |
| Oil quality | Premium (virgin-grade) | Lower (requires full refining) |
| Omega-3 retention | High (≥55% ALA) | Moderate (∼50% ALA after refining) |
| Processing temperature | ≤40°C | ≤110°C |
| Solvent use | None | Hexane |
| Capital cost | Low–moderate | High |
| Operating cost | Moderate | Lower per kg of oil |
| Product types | Virgin + Refined | Refined only |
| By-product value | High (high-fat meal) | Low (defatted meal) |
