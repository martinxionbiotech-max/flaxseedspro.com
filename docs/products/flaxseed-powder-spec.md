## 6. Protein Solubility Curve (pH Profile)

Protein solubility is determined by the nitrogen solubility index (NSI) method: 1 % (w/v) dispersion in deionised water, pH adjusted with 0.1 N HCl / NaOH, stirred 30 min at 25 °C, centrifuged 10,000 × g for 15 min, nitrogen in supernatant by Kjeldahl.

| pH | Protein Solubility (%) — Both Grades | Remarks |
|---|---|---|
| 2.0 | 38 ± 4 | Acidic stimulation — partial hydrolysis |
| 2.5 | 35 ± 4 | |
| 3.0 | 32 ± 4 | |
| 3.5 | 28 ± 3 | |
| 4.0 | 22 ± 3 | |
| **4.5** | **15 ± 3** | **Isoelectric point — minimum solubility** |
| 5.0 | 18 ± 3 | |
| 5.5 | 25 ± 3 | |
| 6.0 | 32 ± 3 | |
| 6.5 | 38 ± 3 | |
| 7.0 | 42 ± 3 | Neutral pH — moderate solubility |
| 7.5 | 46 ± 3 | |
| 8.0 | 49 ± 3 | |
| 8.5 | 52 ± 3 | |
| 9.0 | 54 ± 3 | |
| 9.5 | 55 ± 3 | |
| **10.0** | **55 ± 3** | **Maximum solubility** |

**Shape of Curve:** Classic U-shaped (V-shaped) solubility profile typical of flaxseed globulin (linin) and albumin (conlinin) fractions. Minimum at pH 4.5 (isoelectric region), rising steeply on both acidic and alkaline sides. Solubility at pH 10 (~55 %) is approximately 3.7× the minimum at pH 4.5 (~15 %). The curve is essentially identical for full-fat and defatted grades because the protein fraction is not chemically altered by the defatting process; however, defatted grade disperses more readily due to the absence of oil interference.

## 7. Functional Properties — Hydration & Dispersion

| Property | Unit | FSP-PDR-001 (Full-fat) | FSP-PDR-002 (Defatted) | Method |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Dispersibility Index** | % | ≥ 65 | ≥ 75 | Modified A/S Niro Atomiser |
| **Wettability Time** | sec | 30 – 60 | 15 – 30 | Static wetting 25 °C DI water |
| **Water Absorption Index (WAI)** | g/g | 2.5 – 3.5 | 3.0 – 4.5 | Anderson method |
| **Water Solubility Index (WSI)** | % | 12 – 18 | 18 – 25 | Anderson method |
| **Oil Holding Capacity** | g/g | 1.8 – 2.6 | 1.2 – 1.8 | Centrifugation method |
| **Swelling Capacity** | mL/g | 4.0 – 6.0 | 5.0 – 7.5 | Bed volume method |

**Interpretation:**
- Defatted powder (FSP-PDR-002) wets faster (~2×), exhibits higher dispersibility, greater water absorption, and higher water solubility due to increased exposure of hydrophilic fibre and protein surfaces after oil removal.
- Full-fat powder (FSP-PDR-001) holds more oil (native + added), making it suitable for emulsified and high-fat formulations.
- Both grades form stable dispersions in water under moderate shear (500–1500 rpm, 1–2 min).

## 13. Storage Conditions & Shelf Life

| Condition | FSP-PDR-001 (Full-fat) | FSP-PDR-002 (Defatted) |
|---|---|---|
| **Ambient (≤ 25 °C, ≤ 60 % RH)** | 6 months from production | 12 months from production |
| **Refrigerated (2 – 8 °C)** | 9 months from production | 18 months from production |
| **Frozen (≤ −18 °C)** | 12 months | 24 months |
| **After Opening** | Use within 4 weeks (ambient) | Use within 8 weeks (ambient) |

**Critical Storage Requirements (both grades):**
1. Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources.
2. Keep containers sealed when not in use.
3. Avoid temperature fluctuations exceeding ±10 °C/24 h to prevent moisture migration.
4. Do not store near strong odours (spices, chemicals) — flaxseed powder readily absorbs ambient odours.
5. Once opened, reseal tightly and use before the "use within" period indicated.
6. Always follow FIFO (First In, First Out) rotation.

**Shelf-Life Endpoint Criteria:**
- Peroxide value exceeds 5.0 meq O₂/kg (full-fat) or 3.0 meq O₂/kg (defatted).
- Sensory: noticeable rancid, painty, or fishy odour.
- Colour shift: >5 ΔE (CIE Lab*) from baseline.
- Moisture gain: >1.0 % above specification maximum.
