# Nutritional Studies

---

## Key Research Findings

### Omega-3 & Cardiovascular Health
Multiple clinical studies confirm that daily consumption of 30-50g of flaxseed (or 1-2 tbsp flaxseed oil) provides significant ALA omega-3 intake. The American Heart Association recognizes ALA as beneficial for heart health.

*Key studies:*
- Rodriguez-Leyva et al. (2013) — "Flaxseed on Cardiovascular Health"
- Edel et al. (2015) — "Flaxseed lignans and blood pressure"

### Lignans & Antioxidant Properties
Flaxseeds contain 75-800 times more lignans than any other plant food. SDG (secoisolariciresinol diglucoside) has demonstrated antioxidant and phytoestrogenic properties.

### Dietary Fiber & Digestive Health
With 27-28% total dietary fiber (1/3 soluble, 2/3 insoluble), flaxseed supports digestive regularity and gut microbiome health.

## Recommended Daily Intake

| Application | Recommended | Form |
|:------------|:-----------:|:----:|
| General nutrition | 1-2 tbsp (15-30g) | Ground (milled) |
| Omega-3 supplementation | 1 tbsp oil (15ml) | Oil |
| Fiber intake | 2-3 tbsp (30-45g) | Whole or ground |
| Baking inclusion | 5-15% of flour weight | Milled meal |

## Regulatory Nutrition Claims

- **Source of Omega-3 ALA** — ≥ 0.3g ALA per 100g (EU regulation)
- **High in Omega-3 ALA** — ≥ 0.6g ALA per 100g (EU regulation)
- **High Fiber** — ≥ 6g fiber per 100g
- **Source of Protein** — ≥ 12% energy from protein
