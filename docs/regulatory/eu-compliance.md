# EU Compliance — FlaxseedsPro (European Market Access)

> **Scope:** FlaxseedsPro products (whole flaxseed, milled/ground flaxseed, flaxseed oil) placed on the European Union / European Economic Area market. Covers EU food law, labelling, claims, organic certification, novel foods, and RASFF obligations.

---

## 1. EU General Food Law — Regulation (EC) 178/2002

### 1.1 Foundational Principles

Regulation (EC) 178/2002 establishes the **general principles and requirements of food law** in the EU. It applies to all stages of the food chain, including import.

**Key principles relevant to FlaxseedsPro:**

- **Risk Analysis (Article 6):** EU food law is based on risk assessment (scientific), risk management (policy), and risk communication. FlaxseedsPro must ensure any claims or safety assessments are scientifically substantiated.
- **Precautionary Principle (Article 7):** Where scientific uncertainty exists about a potential health risk, the EU may adopt provisional risk management measures. Relevant for novel flaxseed fractions or high-lignan extracts.
- **Protection of Consumers' Interests (Article 8):** Food must not be misled by labelling, presentation, or advertising.

### 1.2 Traceability — Article 18

**Mandatory one-step-back, one-step-forward traceability** at all stages of production, processing, and distribution:

| Traceability Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Suppliers** | FlaxseedsPro must identify who supplied each lot of flaxseed (name, address, product, quantity, date) |
| **Customers** | FlaxseedsPro must identify who received each product (name, address, date) |
| **Documentation** | Records must be kept for the shelf life + 5 years (EU default) or as specified by national law |
| **Lot Identification** | Each batch/lot of flaxseed products must be clearly identified with a unique code |

### 1.3 RASFF — Rapid Alert System for Food and Feed

- **RASFF (Articles 50–52):** A system for notifying food safety risks across EU member states. If a flaxseed product poses a serious risk to human health, FlaxseedsPro must:
  1. Immediately notify competent authorities of the member state where the product is placed (or withdraw from market)
  2. Co-operate with the RASFF contact point
  3. Implement trace forward/back to identify all affected batches
- **Common RASFF notifications for flaxseed:** Salmonella (in seeds), aflatoxins (rare but monitored), ochratoxin A, undeclared allergens

### 1.4 Food and Feed Business Operator Obligations

As a food business operator (FBO) placing flaxseed on the EU market, FlaxseedsPro must:
- Ensure food safety (Article 14)
- Withdraw unsafe food from the market (Article 19)
- Inform competent authorities of any non-compliance (Article 19)

## 2. EU Hygiene Regulation — Regulation (EC) 852/2004

### 2.1 General Requirements

All EU food businesses must implement:

1. **HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) — Article 5:**
   - Must be based on the **Codex Alimentarius** HACCP principles
   - Documented HACCP plan specific to flaxseed products
   - Critical control points include: metal detection (whole/milled flaxseed), drying to < 10% moisture, temperature control for oil products
   - Seven HACCP principles: hazard analysis, CCP identification, critical limits, monitoring, corrective actions, verification, documentation

2. **Prerequisite Programmes (PRPs) — Annex II:**
   - Facilities must meet structural and hygiene requirements: cleanable surfaces, pest control, waste management, water quality
   - Personnel hygiene: hand-washing facilities, protective clothing, training
   - Pest control: flaxseed is susceptible to stored-product moths (*Ephestia kuehniella*, *Plodia interpunctella*) and beetles (*Tribolium* spp.)
   - Maintenance and cleaning: schedules must be documented and verified

### 2.2 Specific Hygiene Requirements for Flaxseed

| Product Type | Key Hygiene Controls |
|---|---|
| **Whole flaxseed (shelf-stable)** | Incoming inspection, moisture monitoring (< 10%), metal detection, pest exclusion |
| **Milled/ground flaxseed** | Controlled milling environment, allergen cross-contact risk, oxygen-barrier packaging to prevent rancidity |
| **Flaxseed oil (cold-pressed)** | Low-temperature pressing (avoiding oxidation), nitrogen blanketing, opaque packaging, refrigerated storage recommended |

### 2.3 Registration of Food Business

FlaxseedsPro must register each food establishment (facility) with the competent authority of the EU member state where operations are based.

## 3. EU Food Information to Consumers (FIC) — Regulation (EU) 1169/2011

### 3.1 Mandatory Food Information

All prepackaged flaxseed products must display in a **legible, indelible, and easily visible** manner:

| Information Item | Article | Notes for Flaxseed |
|---|---|---|
| Name of the food | Art. 17 | "Flaxseed" or "Linseed"; "Cold-pressed flaxseed oil" |
| List of ingredients | Art. 18 | All ingredients in descending weight order; allergens emphasised |
| Allergens (14 declared) | Art. 21 | Must be highlighted in ingredient list (e.g., **bold**, italics, contrasting colour) |
| Net quantity | Art. 23 | Metric (g, kg, ml, L) |
| Date of minimum durability | Art. 24 | "Best before" for flaxseed (shelf-stable); "Use by" for perishable products |
| Storage conditions | Art. 25 | "Store in a cool, dry place" for seeds; "Refrigerate after opening" for oil |
| Name/address of FBO | Art. 9(1)(h) | EU-based responsible operator (importer or distributor) |
| Country of origin or place of provenance | Art. 26 | Mandatory if omission would mislead; or if required for specific products (see §3.3) |
| Nutrition declaration | Art. 29–31 | Mandatory for all prepackaged foods since 13 December 2016 |
| Lot number | Art. 18 of 178/2002 | Unique lot identification for traceability |

### 3.2 Allergen Labelling — 14 Mandatory Allergens

The FIC Regulation requires declaration of **14 allergen groups** (Annex II):

1. Cereals containing gluten (wheat, rye, barley, oats, spelt, etc.)
2. Crustaceans and products thereof
3. Eggs and products thereof
4. Fish and products thereof
5. Peanuts and products thereof
6. **Soybeans and products thereof** — relevant if flaxseed is processed on shared equipment with soy
7. Milk and products thereof (including lactose)
8. Tree nuts (almonds, hazelnuts, walnuts, cashews, pecans, pistachios, etc.)
9. Celery and products thereof
10. Mustard and products thereof
11. Sesame seeds and products thereof — **relevant** for cross-contact if co-processed
12. Sulphur dioxide and sulphites (> 10 mg/kg or mg/L)
13. **Lupin and products thereof**
14. **Molluscs and products thereof**

> **Note:** Flaxseed itself is **not** one of the 14 mandated allergens under EU law. However, cross-contact from shared facilities or equipment must be declared with precautionary allergen labelling (PAL) such as "May contain traces of..."

### 3.3 Country of Origin Labelling

Article 26 requires country of origin labelling:
- For products where the main ingredient originates from a different country than the product's declared origin
- For fresh, chilled, and frozen meat (specific categories)
- Flaxseed oil and milled flaxseed: if the origin of the raw material (e.g., Canada or Kazakhstan) differs from the processing country, both must be indicated

### 3.4 Nutrition Declaration (Annex XV)

Format on pack (per 100 g or per 100 ml):

| Nutrient | Regulatory Status |
|---|---|
| Energy (kJ / kcal) | Mandatory |
| Fat | Mandatory |
| - of which saturates | Mandatory |
| - **of which mono-unsaturates** | **Voluntary** |
| - **of which polyunsaturates** | **Voluntary** — recommended for flaxseed oil |
| Carbohydrate | Mandatory |
| - of which sugars | Mandatory |
| Protein | Mandatory |
| Salt | Mandatory |
| **Fibre** | **Voluntary** — recommended for whole/milled flaxseed |
| **Vitamins and minerals** | **Voluntary** — only if present in significant amounts (≥ 15% NRV per 100 g) |

**Flaxseed-specific nutrition values (typical per 100 g whole seed):**
- Energy: ~2,234 kJ / 534 kcal
- Fat: ~42 g (saturates ~3.7 g, MUFA ~7.5 g, PUFA ~28.7 g — mainly ALA)
- Carbohydrate: ~29 g (of which sugars ~1.5 g)
- Fibre: ~27 g
- Protein: ~18 g
- Salt: ~0.08 g

## 4. Nutrition & Health Claims — Regulation (EC) 1924/2006

### 4.1 General Requirements

All claims on flaxseed products must comply with the EU Nutrition and Health Claims Regulation (NHCR). Claims must be:
- **Scientifically substantiated** (based on generally accepted scientific evidence)
- **Not ambiguous or misleading**
- **Based on ex-ante authorisation** by the European Commission for health claims

### 4.2 Nutrition Claims Relevant to Flaxseed

Only nutrition claims listed in the **Annex** of Regulation 1924/2006 are permitted:

| Claim | Condition per 100 g | Flaxseed Applicability |
|---|---|---|
| **Source of fibre** | ≥ 3 g fibre | ✓ (~27 g) |
| **High fibre** | ≥ 6 g fibre | ✓ |
| **Source of omega-3 fatty acids** | ≥ 0.3 g ALA | ✓ (~22 g ALA) |
| **High omega-3 fatty acids** | ≥ 0.6 g ALA | ✓ |
| **Source of protein** | ≥ 12% of energy from protein | ✓ (~18 g) |
| **High protein** | ≥ 20% of energy from protein | ✓ |
| **Low saturated fat** | ≤ 1.5 g saturates (< 10% energy) | ✓ (~3.7 g saturates) — check per-serving basis |

### 4.3 Authorised Health Claims (Article 13.1)

Only EFSA-authorised health claims may be used. Relevant claims for flaxseed:

| Claim | Authorised? | Condition of Use |
|---|---|---|
| **ALA contributes to maintenance of normal blood cholesterol levels** | **Yes** (EFSA Journal 2009; 9(4):2059) | Must state benefit is obtained with a daily intake of **2 g ALA**. Information that the claim refers to ALA (not DHA/EPA) |
| **Linoleic acid contributes to maintenance of normal blood cholesterol levels** | Yes | For LA; flaxseed oil low in LA, high in ALA |
| "Helps to maintain normal blood pressure" | Generally not authorised for flaxseed alone | No specific EFSA opinion for flaxseed > BP |
| "Alpha-linolenic acid contributes to maintenance of normal brain function" | Under review / not authorised as generic claim | |

**Important:** Article 13 health claims must be accompanied by:
- The **full authorised wording** as published in EU Register of Nutrition and Health Claims
- A statement on the **importance of a varied, balanced diet**

### 4.4 Nutrient Profiles (Article 4)

The EU Commission has proposed (but not yet adopted as of 2026) nutrient profiles that products must comply with to bear health/nutrition claims. Flaxseed oil is naturally high in fat, which may affect eligibility once profiles are finalised.

## 5. EU Organic Regulation — Regulation (EU) 2018/848

### 5.1 Regulatory Framework

From **1 January 2022**, Regulation (EU) 2018/848 replaced EC 834/2007 and EC 889/2008. Key changes for organic flaxseed:

- **Group certification:** Permitted for smallholder groups under strict rules
- **Mandatory computerised certification system** (TRACES)
- **Imported organic products** must comply with equivalent production rules (or be certified by an EU-recognised control body)

### 5.2 Organic Certification Requirements for Flaxseed

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| **Seed** | Must be organic seed (derogations for non-organic seed expire gradually) |
| **Fertiliser** | Only permitted organic fertilisers (e.g., composted manure, plant-based) |
| **Pest control** | Only authorised plant protection products (e.g., pheromone traps for moths, neem-based products) |
| **Processing** | No irradiation, minimal additives (Annex II of 2018/848), no GMO |
| **Labelling** | EU organic logo required on prepackaged products; code of the control body (e.g., DE-ÖKO-001) |

### 5.3 Equivalency for Imports

Non-EU organic flaxseed can be imported into the EU under:
- **Trade agreements** with recognised third countries (Canada, US under NOP equivalence — note EU-US Organic Equivalence Arrangement)
- **Compliance agreements** with EU-recognised control bodies (e.g., Ceres, ECOCERT, Soil Association Certification)
- **Catch-all provision:** Products certified by an EU-recognised control body in the country of origin

## 6. EU Novel Foods Regulation — Regulation (EU) 2015/2283

### 6.1 Applicability to Flaxseed

**Traditional flaxseed** (whole, milled, cold-pressed oil) is **not** a novel food — it has a history of consumption in the EU prior to 15 May 1997.

**However, the following flaxseed-derived products may be classified as novel foods:**
- **Hydrolysates or protein isolates** from flaxseed — considered novel unless demonstrated safe by traditional use
- **Highly concentrated lignan extracts** (secoisolariciresinol diglucoside — SDG) — classified as novel food; FlaxseedPro's **LinLife SDG** ingredient is a novel food
- **Fermented flaxseed products** or enzymatically modified fractions

### 6.2 Authorisation Process

For novel flaxseed ingredients:
1. Submit a **novel food application** to the European Commission (via the e-submission portal)
2. EFSA conducts a **scientific assessment** (9-month timeline, may be extended)
3. Commission authorises via **implementing regulation** with conditions of use, labelling requirements, and specification
4. **Data protection** (5 years) may be granted for proprietary scientific evidence

### 6.3 Conditions of Use (Example — SDG Flaxseed Extract)

A hypothetical authorisation for a concentrated flaxseed lignan extract would specify:
- Maximum daily intake (e.g., 50 mg SDG)
- Target population (adults, excluding pregnant/nursing women)
- Labelling statement: "Not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women"
- Specification: Purity, SDG content (> 20%), solvent residues, microbiological criteria

## 7. RASFF Notification Process

### 7.1 When to Notify

FlaxseedsPro must notify competent authorities immediately via the **RASFF system** (through the relevant national contact point) when:

| Category | Example for Flaxseed |
|---|---|
| **Serious health risk** | Salmonella detected in a batch; microbiological contamination above legal limits |
| **Border rejection** | Consignment detained at EU border due to elevated aflatoxin/pesticide residues |
| **Food fraud** | Adulteration of flaxseed oil with cheaper oils (e.g., soybean, rapeseed) |

### 7.2 Notification Types

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Alert notification** | Serious risk requiring immediate action (recall, withdrawal) |
| **Information notification** | Risk identified, but other member states need not take immediate action |
| **Border rejection notification** | Consignment rejected at external border; product does not enter EU market |

### 7.3 FlaxseedsPro's Obligations

- Designate a **RASFF contact person**
- Maintain **24/7 availability** for recall/withdrawal communications
- Have a **written recall plan** compatible with RASFF requirements
- Conduct **root cause analysis** for any RASFF notifications involving FlaxseedsPro
- Co-operate with **national competent authorities** (e.g., FSA in UK, ANSES in France, BVL in Germany)

## Summary of Key EU Compliance Actions for FlaxseedsPro

1. **Implement full traceability system** (one-step-back, one-step-forward) per EC 178/2002
2. **Establish HACCP plan** per EC 852/2004 — verified by competent authority
3. **Register food business** in the EU member state of first import
4. **Update product labels** to comply with FIC Regulation 1169/2011 (14 allergens, nutrition declaration, origin)
5. **File claims** only if listed in EU Register of Nutrition and Health Claims (EC 1924/2006)
6. **Obtain organic certification** (EU 2018/848) if marketing organic flaxseed — use recognised control body
7. **Apply for novel food authorisation** (EU 2015/2283) for any non-traditional flaxseed fractions
8. **Designate RASFF contact** and maintain recall readiness
9. **Retain records** for product shelf life + 5 years (as required by national implementation)
