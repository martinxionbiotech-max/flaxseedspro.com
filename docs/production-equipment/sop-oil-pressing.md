# SOP-OIL-PRESSING: Cold Press Oil Extraction

**Document No:** SOP-OP-001  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** 2026-07-20  
**Department:** Production  
**Scope:** This procedure covers cold press oil extraction from flaxseeds using screw press equipment, including solvent extraction safety, plate-and-frame filtration (PPF), and nitrogen generator operation.

---

## 1. Safety

### 1.1 General Safety
- Only trained and authorised personnel may operate the oil press.
- Loose clothing, jewellery, and long hair must be secured.
- Emergency stop buttons are located at both ends of the press and the control panel.
- Ensure all guards and interlocks are in place before operation.

### 1.2 Solvent Extraction Safety (ATEX / Explosion Prevention)
- The solvent extraction area is classified ATEX Zone 1 (hazardous).
- **LEL Monitoring:** Continuous Lower Explosive Limit (LEL) monitoring must be active. If LEL exceeds 25% of the lower limit, the system automatically shuts down. Evacuate immediately.
- **Earthing/Bonding:** All equipment, containers, and transfer lines must be electrically bonded and grounded before solvent handling. Verify resistance <10 ohms.
- No smoking, open flames, or non-Ex-rated electronic devices.
- Only Ex-rated tools and equipment permitted within Zone 1.
- Solvent storage must be in grounded, double-walled containers with secondary containment.

### 1.3 Hot Work / Mechanical Work
- Hot work permit required for any welding, grinding, or electrical work within 15 m of solvent areas.
- Equipment must be gas-freed and certified before maintenance.

### 1.4 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- Safety glasses, chemical-resistant gloves (nitrile), and steel-toed boots.
- Hearing protection required near press and motor areas (>85 dBA).
- Solvent handling: full-face shield, acid-resistant apron, and solvent-resistant gloves.

## 2. Pre-Start Checks / Pre-Press Kernel Quality Check

### 2.1 Kernel Quality Inspection
- Sample each batch of flaxseed kernels before pressing.
- **Acceptance criteria:**
  - Moisture content: 6–9% (by rapid moisture analyser)
  - Foreign material: <2% w/w
  - Free fatty acids (FFA): ≤1.5%
  - Peroxide value (PV): <5 meq O₂/kg
  - Visual inspection: no mould, insect damage, or off-odour
- Reject any batch not meeting criteria. Document rejection in production log.

### 2.2 Equipment Checks
- Verify barrel temperature is <30°C (ambient).
- Check oil collection tank is clean and dry.
- Verify N₂ generator supply pressure (4–6 bar).
- Confirm cooling water circulation is on and at 15–20°C.
- Inspect screw shaft and barrel for wear or damage.

---

## 3. Startup Sequence

### 3.1 N₂ Generator Operation
1. Open N₂ supply valve at source.
2. Power on N₂ generator control panel.
3. Verify N₂ purity ≥99.5% via oxygen analyser.
4. Set outlet pressure to 2.5 bar.
5. Purge oil collection tank headspace with N₂ for 3 minutes before feed.

### 3.2 Press Startup
1. Start cooling water pump; verify flow ≥20 L/min.
2. Turn press main power ON.
3. Set screw speed to **low speed** (10–15 RPM).
4. **Warm-up phase:** Run at low speed for 5 minutes with no feed. Monitor bearing and motor temperature.
5. Begin feeding kernels at **50% feed rate** for 5 minutes.
6. Gradually increase feed rate to **75%** for 5 minutes, then to **100% full feed**.
7. Verify barrel temperature remains stable (<40°C). If temp rises >40°C, reduce feed rate immediately.

### 3.3 Temperature Monitoring (Critical)
- Barrel temperature must never exceed **40°C**.
- Check temperature at three zones: feed zone, compression zone, discharge zone.
- Log temperature every 15 minutes during production.
- **If temperature exceeds 40°C:**
  1. Reduce feed rate to 50%.
  2. Increase cooling water flow.
  3. If temp does not drop within 5 minutes, shut down (see Section 5).

## 4. PPF (Plate-and-Frame Filtration) Operation

### 4.1 Inspection of Filter Plates and Cloths
- Before each run, inspect all filter plates for cracks, warping, or damage.
- Check filter cloths for holes, excessive wear, or blinding.
- Replace cloths if any defect is found or after 10 production cycles.

### 4.2 Filter Cloth Replacement
1. Remove old cloth and clean plate surface thoroughly.
2. Cut new cloth to size with 20 mm overhang on each side.
3. Centre cloth on plate and secure with retaining pins.
4. Verify cloth is smooth, no wrinkles or folds.
5. Tighten frame bolts evenly in a star pattern to 15 N·m.

### 4.3 Filtration Startup
1. Close all drain valves.
2. Open feed valve to start oil flow into PPF.
3. Vent air from top of filter press.
4. Gradually increase pressure to 2 bar max.
5. Check filtrate clarity — if cloudy, recirculate to feed tank.

## 5. Normal Shutdown Sequence

### 5.1 Press Shutdown
1. **Reduce feed:** Stop kernel feed 10 minutes before shutdown.
2. **Flush:** Continue running press at low speed (10 RPM) to clear remaining material from barrel. Add flush material (e.g., spent flax meal) if needed.
3. **Cool down:** Run at low speed for 10 minutes with cooling water ON.
4. When barrel temperature drops to <30°C, turn off screw drive.
5. Stop cooling water pump after 5-minute post-circulation.
6. Turn off main power.

### 5.2 N₂ Generator Shutdown
1. Close N₂ supply valve to oil tank.
2. Allow generator to idle for 2 minutes.
3. Power off N₂ generator.
4. Close main N₂ source valve.

### 5.3 PPF Shutdown
1. Close feed valve.
2. Open drain valves to release residual oil.
3. Vent remaining pressure.
4. If cloths will be idle >24 hours, remove and wash.

## 6. CIP (Clean-in-Place) Procedure

### 6.1 Frequency
- After each production batch (or every 8 hours of continuous operation).
- Between product type changes.

### 6.2 CIP Steps
1. **Cold water rinse** (10 min): Circulate cold water through press at low speed to remove loose solids. Drain.
2. **Hot alkaline wash** (20 min): Circulate 2% NaOH solution at 60–70°C at low speed. Do not exceed 70°C.
3. **Hot water rinse** (10 min): Circulate hot water (60°C) until pH of rinse water is neutral (pH 6–8).
4. **Sanitisation** (15 min): Circulate 100 ppm peracetic acid solution at ambient temperature.
5. **Final rinse** (5 min): Circulate potable cold water. Drain.
6. Dry barrel by running press at low speed with no feed for 5 minutes.

### 6.3 PPF CIP
1. Disassemble filter press.
2. Remove cloths and wash separately in hot alkaline solution.
3. Hand brush each plate with soft nylon brush.
4. Rinse with hot water and air dry.
5. Reassemble with clean cloths.

## 7. Troubleshooting

| Problem | Possible Cause | Corrective Action |
|---|---|---|
| **High barrel temperature (>40°C)** | Feed rate too high | Reduce feed rate; increase cooling water flow |
|  | Cooling water flow low | Check pump and valves; verify water temperature <20°C |
|  | Screw speed too high | Reduce RPM to 10–15 |
|  | Friction due to worn screw/barrel | Inspect and replace worn parts |
| **Low oil yield** | Low kernel oil content | Verify kernel quality per Section 2.1 |
|  | Moisture too high/low | Condition kernels to 6–9% moisture |
|  | Screw speed incorrect | Adjust to optimum 15–20 RPM |
|  | Worn barrel or screw profile | Schedule maintenance for re-profiling |
| **Dark / high-colour oil** | Barrel temperature >40°C | Reduce temperature immediately |
|  | Over-pressing (too much material) | Reduce feed rate |
|  | Long residence time | Increase screw speed slightly |
| **High Peroxide Value (PV)** | High temperature exposure | Ensure all operations below 40°C |
|  | Oxygen exposure during collection | Verify N₂ blanket is active and pure |
|  | Old or degraded kernels | Test FFA and PV of incoming kernels |
|  | Long holding time in heated tank | Reduce hold time; increase N₂ purge |
| **Cloudy filtrate (PPF)** | Torn or worn filter cloth | Replace cloth |
|  | Pressure too high causing bypass | Reduce to 2 bar max |
|  | Gasket leak between plates | Re-tighten frame bolts; replace gaskets |
| **N₂ purity below 99.5%** | Feed air pressure low | Check compressor; adjust to 4–6 bar |
|  | Membrane/PSA bed saturated | Replace membrane/regenerate bed |
|  | Oxygen sensor calibration drift | Recalibrate per manufacturer spec |

## 8. Emergency Shutdown

- Press any **emergency stop** button for immediate shutdown.
- For solvent fire: activate fixed CO₂ fire suppression system. **Do not use water.**
- Evacuate area; sound alarm; call emergency response.
- For N₂ leak: ventilate area; do not enter low-oxygen atmosphere without SCBA.
