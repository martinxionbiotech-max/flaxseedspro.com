# SOP: Dehulling Equipment

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Standard Operating Procedure covers the operation, adjustment, and maintenance of the dehulling equipment train, including the roller mill (flaking/cracking rolls), air classifier, aspiration system, and associated conveyors. It applies to all production operators and maintenance personnel handling flaxseed dehulling operations.

## 2. Safety Precautions

### 2.1 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- **Mandatory:** Ear protection (hearing protectors) — noise levels near roller mill exceed 90 dB(A)
- **Mandatory:** Safety glasses with side shields
- **Mandatory:** Steel-toed safety boots
- **Mandatory:** Dust mask (N95 or equivalent) — hull dust is airborne
- **Required (when handling lubricants):** Nitrile gloves

### 2.2 Lockout / Tagout (LOTO)
- **Lockout required before:** Any roller gap adjustment, roll change, air classifier entry, conveyor maintenance
- **Lockout required before:** Cleaning any equipment interior
- Verify zero energy state — disconnect and lock out main power
- Test restart (press START) after lockout to confirm no movement
- Personal lock + tag with name, date, and reason
- LOTO removal only after all personnel are clear and tools removed

### 2.3 Hot Surfaces
- Roller mill bearings and gearbox can reach 70 °C during operation
- Allow minimum 15 minutes cool-down before maintenance

### 2.4 Rotating & Pinch Points
- Roller mill rolls create a dangerous pinch point — NEVER reach into the nip during operation
- Air classifier rotor has high inertia — wait for full stop (min 5 min coast-down) before opening

### 2.5 General
- No loose clothing, jewelry, or unrestrained hair near rotating shafts
- Ensure all belt guards and interlock switches are in place and functional
- Arc flash PPE (rated for category) required when opening electrical panels

## 3. Pre-Start Checks

Before starting the dehulling line, complete the following checklist. Each item must be verified and initialed.

- [ ] **PPE**: Ear protection, dust mask, safety glasses, steel-toed boots worn
- [ ] **Guards & Interlocks**: All safety guards in place; interlock switches functional
- [ ] **Roller Mill Gap**: Gap set to correct value per product specification (see Section 5)
- [ ] **Rotor Balance Log**: Rotor balance check performed within the last week (weekly requirement)
- [ ] **Air Classifier**: Rotor spins freely (by hand check with power locked out); vane settings at target position
- [ ] **Air Classifier Velocity**: Calibration verified within last shift — air velocity within ±2 % of setpoint
- [ ] **Aspiration System**: Fan running, airflow within normal range, ducting clear
- [ ] **Conveyor Path**: All conveyors/elevators clear of obstructions; belts tracking correctly
- [ ] **Feed Hopper**: Material level adequate; no bridging or foreign objects
- [ ] **Emergency Stops**: All E-stops tested and functional
- [ ] **Product Changeover**: If switching seed types, cleaning procedure (Section 8) completed
- [ ] **Electrical Panel**: All MCC breakers in ON position; no alarm indicators

## 4. Startup Sequence

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Confirm all Pre-Start Checks are completed and initialed |
| 2 | Announce startup over plant intercom: "Starting dehulling line" |
| 3 | Start aspiration system (dust collection / air classifier fan) — verify airflow |
| 4 | Start discharge conveyors (hulls and meats conveyors) |
| 5 | Start air classifier rotor — allow to reach full speed (verify via tachometer) |
| 6 | Start roller mill — run at no-load for 30 seconds to verify smooth operation |
| 7 | Verify roller mill no-load amperage is within spec (typically 40–60 % of full load) |
| 8 | Start feed system at minimum rate (5–10 % of max) |
| 9 | Observe product discharge: check for proper hull/meat separation |
| 10 | Adjust air classifier velocity to target setpoint for current seed type |
| 11 | Increase feed rate to target operating rate in 25 % increments — stabilize 30 seconds between increments |
| 12 | Verify roller mill amperage, air classifier rotor amperage, and aspiration airflow at each step |
| 13 | Log startup in equipment logbook: time, operator name, feed rate, roll gap setting, classifier velocity |

## 5. Normal Operation Parameters

| Parameter | Normal Range | Alarm Limit | Shutdown Limit |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| Roller mill gap (flaxseed) | 0.3–0.6 mm | >0.7 mm or <0.2 mm | >0.8 mm or <0.1 mm |
| Roller mill amperage | 25–40 A | >45 A | >55 A |
| Roll mill roll speed (fast) | 400–500 RPM | >520 RPM | >550 RPM |
| Roll speed differential | 1.5:1 to 2.5:1 | Outside range | — |
| Air classifier rotor speed | 800–1200 RPM | >1300 RPM | >1400 RPM |
| Air classifier air velocity | 3.5–5.5 m/s | >6.0 m/s or <3.0 m/s | >6.5 m/s or <2.5 m/s |
| Aspiration airflow | 3000–4000 m³/h | <2700 or >4300 | <2500 or >4500 |
| Feed rate (flaxseed) | 800–1200 kg/h | >1400 kg/h | >1600 kg/h |
| Hull content (in meats fraction) | <5 % | >8 % | >10 % |
| Whole seed in hulls fraction | <2 % | >4 % | >6 % |
| Roller mill bearing temp | 40–65 °C | >70 °C | >80 °C |
| Air classifier rotor bearing temp | 35–55 °C | >60 °C | >70 °C |

## 6. Shutdown Sequence

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Reduce feed rate to minimum (5–10 % of max) |
| 2 | Stop feed system |
| 3 | Continue running roller mill and air classifier for 2 minutes to clear remaining product |
| 4 | Stop roller mill |
| 5 | Stop air classifier — allow rotor to coast down completely (5+ minutes) before opening |
| 6 | Stop aspiration system |
| 7 | Allow conveyors to run until clear, then stop |
| 8 | Perform visual inspection of rolls (when fully stopped): check for wear, scoring, or buildup |
| 9 | If switching seed types (e.g., brown flax → golden flax), proceed immediately to Section 8 (Cleaning) |
| 10 | Log shutdown in equipment logbook: time, operator name, run hours, product type, any issues |

## 7. Emergency Shutdown Procedure

### Activation
Press any red Emergency Stop (E-Stop). All drives are cut immediately.

### Upon Emergency Shutdown
1. **Do NOT reset** until the cause is identified and corrected
2. Announce over intercom: "Emergency shutdown — dehulling line stopped"
3. Identify cause:
   - Roll jam / foreign object in roller mill
   - Mechanical failure (bearing, belt, shaft)
   - Fire or smoke
   - Personnel injury
   - Electrical fault / motor overload
4. Secure area — prevent re-entry
5. For roll jam: LOTO equipment before attempting any roll gap opening or clearance
6. Correct the cause (see Troubleshooting Section 9)
7. Clear all personnel before resetting E-stops
8. Follow full Startup Sequence (Section 4) after ESD

### Fire Response
- Activate E-stop
- Do NOT open roller mill enclosure — oxygen feeds fire
- Use Class D fire extinguisher for metal fires; CO₂ for electrical
- Evacuate area; notify plant emergency coordinator

## 8. Cleaning Procedure

### 8.1 Between Seed Types (Changeover Cleaning)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Complete full Shutdown Sequence (Section 6) |
| 2 | LOTO all equipment in dehulling line |
| 3 | Open access doors on roller mill, air classifier, and all conveyors |
| 4 | Vacuum clean all interior surfaces — remove all residual seed, hulls, and fines |
| 5 | Roller mill: Brush rolls with stiff nylon brush (no metal brushes — will damage roll surface) |
| 6 | Air classifier: Wipe down rotor vanes, interior walls, and inlet/outlet ducts |
| 7 | Aspiration ducting: Vacuum or blow out with compressed air |
| 8 | Inspect roll surfaces for wear or damage — record findings |
| 9 | Verify no cleaning tools or rags left inside equipment |
| 10 | Close all access doors |
| 11 | Remove LOTO |
| 12 | Adjust roller mill gap and air classifier velocity for new seed type per spec sheet |
| 13 | Proceed to Pre-Start Checks (Section 3) |

### 8.2 Daily Cleaning

- Vacuum floor area around roller mill and air classifier
- Wipe exterior surfaces of all equipment
- Empty aspiration / dust collection bins
- Check and clean feed hopper screen

### 8.3 Deep Cleaning (Weekly or after 100 operating hours)

| Component | Method |
|-----------|--------|
| Roller mill rolls | Remove rolls (with maintenance team), clean with approved solvent (food-grade), inspect for surface defects, measure roll diameter |
| Air classifier | Full disassembly (with maintenance team), clean rotor and stator vanes, check vane wear, verify clearance |
| Aspiration ducts | Disconnect and rod-out all duct runs; check for buildup and blockages |
| Feed hopper & magnet | Clean magnet assembly; wipe down hopper interior |

### 8.4 Chemical Cleaning (As Needed)

| Chemical | Concentration | Contact Time | Rinse Temp | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-------------|------------|-------|
| Food-grade degreaser | 2–5 % v/v | 10–15 min | 50–60 °C | For oily residue on rolls |
| Isopropyl alcohol (99 %) | Neat | Wipe & dry | N/A | Electrical contacts and sensors |

## 9. Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Probable Cause | Remedy | Reference |
|---------|---------------|--------|-----------|
| Incomplete dehulling (high whole seeds) | Roll gap too wide | Reduce gap by 0.05 mm increments; test | Section 5, Gap adjustment procedure |
| Excessive broken seeds / fines | Roll gap too narrow or rolls worn | Increase gap; inspect roll condition | Section 5 |
| High hull content in meats fraction | Air classifier velocity too high | Reduce air velocity by 0.2 m/s steps | Section 5, velocity calibration |
| Seed loss in hulls fraction | Air classifier velocity too low | Increase air velocity by 0.2 m/s steps | Section 5 |
| Roller mill vibration | Rotor imbalance; worn bearings | Perform rotor balance check (weekly); replace bearings if needed | Section 10 PM |
| Roller mill amperage >55 A | Roll gap too tight; feed rate too high; foreign object | Stop immediately; check gap and feed rate; inspect for foreign object | Section 6 / 7 |
| Air classifier rotor won't reach speed | VFD fault; mechanical binding | Check VFD parameters; manually rotate rotor (LOTO first) | Section 3 |
| Aspiration airflow low | Duct blockage; fan belt slipping; filter clogged | Inspect ducts; tighten/replace fan belt; clean filters | Section 8.3 |
| Poor separation after seed type change | Incomplete cleaning from previous run | Deep clean per Section 8.3 before restart | Section 8 |
| Bearing temperature >70 °C | Over-greased or under-greased; seal failure | Check grease volume; replace seal if leaking | Section 5 / 10 |
| Excessive noise from air classifier | Rotor contacting stator; vane loose | Stop immediately; LOTO; inspect and adjust clearance | Section 9 |

## 10. Preventive Maintenance

| Task | Frequency | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Rotor balance check | Weekly | Use vibration analyzer; imbalance <1.0 mm/s RMS |
| Air classifier velocity calibration | Weekly | Verify with pitot tube / anemometer at reference point |
| Roller mill gap verification | Daily (start of shift) | Measure with feeler gauge — adjust per product spec |
| Roll surface inspection | Weekly | Check for wear, scoring, pitting — record roll diameter |
| Bearing greasing (roller mill) | Every 200 operating hours | NLGI #2 grease, 2 pumps per zerk |
| Bearing greasing (air classifier) | Every 200 operating hours | NLGI #2 grease, 1 pump per zerk |
| Belt tension check | Every 300 operating hours | Deflection test per manufacturer spec |
| Roller mill gearbox oil change | Every 2000 operating hours | ISO VG 220 gear oil |
| Air classifier vane wear check | Monthly | Measure vane thickness — replace if <60 % of original |
| Aspiration duct inspection | Monthly | Visual + drop-light inspection for buildup |
| Motor insulation test | Annually | Megger at 500 V; min 1 MΩ |
| Roll replacement | When diameter ≤95 % of new | Replace matched pairs only |
| Full overhaul | Annually or 4000 operating hours | Bearings, belts, rolls, seals, full calibration |
