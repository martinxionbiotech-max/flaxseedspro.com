# SOP-GUM-EXTRACTION: Flaxseed Gum Extraction

**Document No:** SOP-GE-001  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** 2026-07-20  
**Department:** Production  
**Scope:** This procedure covers aqueous ethanol-based gum extraction from flaxseed meal, including extraction tank loading, pH adjustment, temperature ramping, centrifugation, spray drying, and ethanol recovery column operation.

---

## 1. Safety

### 1.1 Ethanol Handling Safety (Flammable)
- Ethanol used in this process is **highly flammable** (flash point ~13°C).
- **Flammable zone classification:** ATEX Zone 2 around extraction tanks and recovery column.
- **No smoking, open flames, or hot work** within 10 m.
- All electrical equipment must be ATEX-certified (Ex-rated).
- Static-dissipative flooring and grounding of all conductive equipment required.

### 1.2 Earthing / Bonding
- All vessels, transfer lines, drums, and pumps must be electrically bonded and earth-grounded.
- Verify ground continuity before transfer operations. Maximum resistance: <10 ohms.
- Use conductive hoses with grounding wire for ethanol transfers.
- Drums must sit on grounded spill pallets.

### 1.3 Ventilation
- Extraction room must have mechanical ventilation with minimum 6 air changes per hour.
- LEL monitors installed at low points; alarm at 25% LEL.
- If LEL alarm sounds: stop all transfer operations, activate exhaust, evacuate.

### 1.4 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- **Hands:** Solvent-resistant gloves (nitrile, neoprene, or Viton). Inspect for pinholes before use.
- **Eyes:** Chemical splash goggles (not just safety glasses). Full-face shield for high-risk activities.
- **Body:** Flame-resistant lab coat or apron. No synthetic fabrics.
- **Feet:** Steel-toed, chemical-resistant boots.
- **Respiratory:** If ethanol vapour detected (<25% LEL), half-face respirator with organic vapour cartridge required.

### 1.5 Spill Response
- Ethanol spill kit located at extraction tank area (absorbent pads, neutralising agent, disposal bags).
- Spill >1 L: evacuate area, ventilate, contain using non-sparking tools.

## 2. Extraction Tank Loading Procedure

### 2.1 Pre-Operational Checks
- Verify extraction tank is clean and free of residual material.
- Check all valves in correct position (closed except tank vent open).
- Confirm temperature probe and pH probe are calibrated.
- Verify ethanol supply tank level and concentration (≥95% ethanol).

### 2.2 Loading Sequence
1. **Weigh meal:** Load pre-weighed flaxseed meal into extraction tank.
2. **Add aqueous ethanol:** Charge extraction solvent (ethanol : water, 60:40 v/v) at a ratio of 10:1 solvent-to-meal (w/w).
3. **Agitation:** Start agitator at low speed (50–100 RPM).
4. **pH adjustment:**
   - Measure initial pH of slurry.
   - Adjust to pH 6.5–7.0 using 1M NaOH or 1M HCl as needed.
   - Add slowly at the tank inlet while agitator is running.
   - Recheck pH after 2 minutes; record on extraction log.
5. Close tank vent once loading is complete.
6. Verify all connections are secure before heating.

## 3. Temperature Ramping and Hold

### 3.1 Heating Parameters
- **Ramp rate:** 1.5°C per minute (do not exceed 2°C/min).
- **Target temperature:** 65–70°C.
- **Hold time:** 90 minutes at target temperature ± 2°C.
- **Maximum temperature:** 75°C (degradation risk above this).

### 3.2 Procedure
1. Set jacket temperature setpoint 5°C above target (70–75°C).
2. Monitor internal slurry temperature continuously.
3. Once slurry reaches 65°C, begin 90-minute hold timer.
4. Record temperature every 15 minutes on **EXTR-LOG-001**.
5. At end of hold, begin cooling ramp (cooling water through jacket) to 40°C.
6. Proceed to centrifugation only when slurry is <40°C.

### 3.3 Alarms
- High temperature alarm set at 72°C — automatically reduces heating.
- High-high alarm at 75°C — shuts off heating and opens cooling water.

## 4. Centrifuge Operation

### 4.1 Pre-Start Checks
- Verify centrifuge bowl is clean and balanced.
- Check that solids discharge mechanism is functional.
- Confirm discharge chute/container is in place.

### 4.2 Startup
1. Close centrifuge door/lid; verify interlock is engaged.
2. Start centrifuge at low speed (500 RPM).
3. Gradually ramp to operating speed (3,000–5,000 RPM depending on model).
4. Verify vibration levels are within tolerance (<5 mm/s displacement).
5. **Start feed pump** at low rate (10 L/min).
6. Gradually increase feed rate to target (20–40 L/min, per process specification).

### 4.3 Solids Discharge Frequency
- **Standard cycle:** Discharge solids every 30 minutes.
- Adjust frequency based on solids loading — if torque exceeds 60%, discharge immediately.
- **Discharge procedure:**
  1. Stop feed pump.
  2. Reduce centrifuge speed to 500 RPM.
  3. Open solids discharge valve.
  4. Run discharge cycle (10 seconds).
  5. Close valve, ramp back to operating speed.
  6. Re-start feed pump.

### 4.4 Shutdown
1. Stop feed pump.
2. Run centrifuge with water flush for 5 minutes.
3. Reduce speed to 500 RPM.
4. Turn off centrifuge.
5. Open lid/door and remove solids.
6. Clean bowl with warm water and soft brush.

## 5. Spray Dryer Operation

### 5.1 Pre-Start Checks
- Verify drying chamber is clean.
- Check atomiser wheel/nozzle condition.
- Confirm inlet and outlet air filters are clean.
- Verify product collection hopper and cyclone are empty.

### 5.2 Startup Sequence
1. **Close product collection valve** (recirculation mode).
2. **Set inlet temperature** to target (180°C for gum drying).
3. **Ignite/heat the air heater:**
   - For gas-fired: verify gas supply pressure, purge for 3 minutes, verify flame scanner.
   - Ensure pilot flame is established before opening main fuel valve.
4. **Establish airflow:** Start main fan at 30% capacity, ramp to 100% over 2 minutes.
5. **Monitor outlet temperature:** Target 80–85°C.
6. **Start feed:** Open feed valve to 10% rate initially.
7. **Observe outlet temperature:** It will drop; allow to stabilise.
8. Gradually increase feed rate to achieve target outlet temperature.
9. **Switch to production mode:** Open product collection valve once steady state is reached.

### 5.3 Shutdown
1. Stop feed pump.
2. Flush feed line with water for 2 minutes.
3. Reduce inlet temperature setpoint to 100°C.
4. Continue running fan until outlet temperature drops below 50°C.
5. Turn off air heater.
6. Run fan for additional 10 minutes (cool down).
7. Stop fan.
8. Collect residual powder from cyclone and chamber.

## 6. Ethanol Recovery Column Operation

### 6.1 Pre-Start Checks
- Verify column is free of obstructions.
- Check reflux pump, condenser cooling water flow.
- Confirm ethanol receiver tank is empty and grounded.

### 6.2 Startup
1. Open cooling water to condenser (flow 15 L/min, inlet temp ≤25°C).
2. Start feed pump at low rate.
3. **Heat via reboiler:** Gradually increase steam/heat input.
4. Monitor top temperature — target 78–82°C (ethanol-water azeotrope).
5. Maintain **reflux ratio** at 2:1 (reflux : distillate) during stabilisation.
6. Allow column to stabilise for 20–30 minutes.

### 6.3 Product Draw
1. Once top temperature is stable and ethanol concentration by GC/online analyser is ≥95%, open distillate draw valve.
2. Maintain reflux ratio at 3:1 for high-purity draw.
3. Monitor bottom temperature — target 100–105°C (water removal).
4. Record temperature, flow, and purity every 30 minutes.

### 6.4 Shutdown
1. Stop feed pump.
2. Continue reflux for 10 minutes.
3. Close distillate draw valve.
4. Turn off reboiler heat.
5. Allow column to cool to <40°C.
6. Stop condenser cooling water.
7. Drain receiver and bottoms.

## 7. Cleaning (Between Batches)

### 7.1 Extraction Tank
1. Drain all residual slurry.
2. Rinse with warm water (40°C).
3. Fill with 2% NaOH solution, circulate for 20 minutes.
4. Drain and rinse with potable water until pH neutral.
5. Final rinse with deionised water.

### 7.2 Spray Dryer
1. Run CIP spray balls for 15 minutes with hot water (60°C).
2. Follow with 1% citric acid rinse.
3. Final hot water rinse.
4. Air dry with fan running, inlet temperature 50°C, for 10 minutes.

### 7.3 Centrifuge
1. Flush with hot water (50°C) at low speed.
2. Disassemble bowl weekly for thorough manual cleaning.
3. Inspect seals; replace if damaged.

### 7.4 Ethanol Recovery Column
- CIP every 5 batches or when pressure drop exceeds 200 mbar.
- Circulate hot water (70°C) through column for 30 minutes.
- Acid clean with 1% phosphoric acid if fouling visible.

## 8. Troubleshooting

| Problem | Possible Cause | Corrective Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Low viscosity of gum solution** | Extraction temperature too low | Verify temp was maintained at 65–70°C for full 90 min |
|  | Short hold time | Ensure 90 min hold was completed |
|  | pH outside 6.5–7.0 range | Recalibrate pH probe; re-check adjustment procedure |
|  | Solvent ratio incorrect | Verify solvent:meal ratio 10:1 |
|  | Meal quality degraded | Check incoming meal FFA and moisture |
| **High protein in gum** | pH too low (isoelectric precipitation of protein) | Keep pH at 6.5–7.0 |
|  | Centrifuge speed too low | Increase to 4,000–5,000 RPM |
|  | Feed rate too high during centrifugation | Reduce to ≤30 L/min |
|  | Solids discharge frequency too low | Increase to every 20 min |
| **Poor solubility of dried gum** | Spray dryer outlet temperature too high | Reduce inlet temp; increase feed rate to lower outlet temp to 80°C |
|  | Over-drying / low moisture | Target 5–8% moisture in dried gum |
|  | Atomisation poor (large droplets) | Check atomiser wheel speed / nozzle condition |
|  | Ethanol residual too high | Verify recovery column purity ≥95% |
| **Low gum yield** | Incomplete extraction | Verify hold time and temperature; consider second extraction |
|  | Loss in centrifuge overflow | Check centrifuge operating conditions |
|  | Spray dryer losses | Check cyclone efficiency and bag filter condition |
| **Ethanol recovery column poor separation** | Reflux ratio too low | Increase to 3:1 |
|  | Reboiler temperature too low | Increase steam input; check bottom temp 100–105°C |
|  | Flooding (high pressure drop) | Reduce feed rate; column may need cleaning |
|  | Cooling water flow insufficient | Verify condenser water flow ≥15 L/min at ≤25°C |

## 9. Emergency Procedures

- **Ethanol spill:** Evacuate, ventilate, contain using non-sparking tools, absorb with spill pads.
- **Ethanol fire:** Use CO₂ or dry chemical extinguisher. **Do not use water.**
- **Centrifuge vibration alarm:** Emergency stop, do not open until bowl fully stops.
- **Spray dryer fire:** Close air inlet, activate CO₂ suppression, stop feed.
