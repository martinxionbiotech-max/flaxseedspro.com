# SOP: Cleaning & Grading Equipment

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Standard Operating Procedure covers the operation, cleaning, and maintenance of the cleaning and grading line, including magnetic separators, screening decks, and the color sorter. It applies to all production operators and maintenance personnel handling flaxseed cleaning and grading operations.

## 2. Safety Precautions

### 2.1 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- **Mandatory:** Ear protection (hearing protectors / earplugs) — noise levels exceed 85 dB(A) during operation
- **Mandatory:** Dust mask (N95 or equivalent) — airborne dust and fines present
- **Mandatory:** Safety glasses with side shields
- **Mandatory:** Steel-toed safety boots
- **Required (when handling chemicals):** Nitrile gloves, chemical splash goggles

### 2.2 Lockout / Tagout (LOTO)
- **Lockout required before:** Any entry into the color sorter, screen deck changes, magnet cleaning, or any maintenance task
- Verify zero energy state: disconnect main power, lock out with personal lock, tag with name/date/reason
- Test that equipment cannot be restarted after lockout (push START button — no movement)
- Remove lock only when work is complete and all personnel are clear

### 2.3 Confined Space / Enclosed Equipment
- The color sorter enclosure is a potential confined space — do NOT enter without:
  - Confined space permit
  - Continuous oxygen monitoring
  - Attendant stationed outside
  - Rescue equipment on-site

### 2.4 Hot Surfaces & Moving Parts
- Motors and gearboxes may reach surface temperatures of 60 °C — allow to cool before contact
- Keep hands, loose clothing, and tools away from rotating screen drives and belt conveyors

### 2.5 General
- No jewelry, loose clothing, or unrestrained long hair near rotating equipment
- Ensure all guards and interlock switches are in place before restarting
- Follow all plant-specific LOTO procedures in addition to this SOP

## 3. Pre-Start Checks

Before starting the cleaning and grading line, complete the following checklist. Each item must be verified and initialed.

- [ ] **PPE**: Ear protection, dust mask, safety glasses, and steel-toed boots worn
- [ ] **Guards & Interlocks**: All safety guards secured, interlock switches functional, no bypassed
- [ ] **Magnet Audit Log**: Last magnet cleaning logged — magnet cleaning must be performed at least every 4 hours
- [ ] **Screen Condition**: Screen decks visually inspected — no tears, holes, or blinding >10%
- [ ] **Screen Swap Log**: Last screen swap recorded — must occur every 500 operating hours
- [ ] **Color Sorter**: Cabin door sealed, compressed air supply on (min 6 bar), air filter dryers drained
- [ ] **Color Sorter Calibration**: Calibration check performed within the last 7 days (weekly requirement)
- [ ] **Dust Collection**: Dust collector system running, filters clean, airflow at normal range
- [ ] **Conveyor Paths**: All belts, elevators, and augers clear of obstructions
- [ ] **Emergency Stops**: All E-stops tested and functional
- [ ] **Product Flow Path**: Discharge spouts aligned to correct destination bin/tote

## 4. Startup Sequence

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Confirm all Pre-Start Checks are completed and initialed |
| 2 | Announce startup over plant intercom: "Starting cleaning and grading line" |
| 3 | Start dust collection system — verify negative pressure at all pickup points |
| 4 | Start discharge conveyors (bins/totes receiving graded product) |
| 5 | Start the screening deck at lowest speed setting |
| 6 | Gradually ramp screening deck to operating speed (check manufacturer spec for RPM) |
| 7 | Start the magnetic separator / magnet housing conveyor |
| 8 | Open compressed air supply to color sorter — verify pressure at 6–8 bar |
| 9 | Start the color sorter — allow 60-second self-test / warm-up cycle |
| 10 | Start feed system at minimum rate (10 % of max) |
| 11 | Observe product flow: confirm even distribution across screen width |
| 12 | Check color sorter rejection pattern — verify no good product lost in reject stream |
| 13 | Increase feed rate to target operating rate — verify all downstream equipment keeps up |
| 14 | Log startup in equipment logbook: time, operator name, feed rate |

## 5. Normal Operation Parameters

| Parameter | Normal Range | Alarm Limit | Shutdown Limit |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| Screen deck speed | 180–220 RPM | >230 RPM or <170 RPM | >250 RPM or <150 RPM |
| Screen vibration amplitude | 3–5 mm | <2.5 mm or >5.5 mm | <2 mm or >6 mm |
| Magnet surface field strength | ≥8000 Gauss | <7500 Gauss | <7000 Gauss |
| Color sorter compressed air | 6–8 bar | <5.5 bar or >8.5 bar | <5 bar or >9 bar |
| Color sorter cabin temp | 20–35 °C | >40 °C | >50 °C |
| Dust collector airflow | 2000–2500 m³/h | <1800 m³/h | <1500 m³/h |
| Feed rate (flaxseed) | 1500–2000 kg/h | >2200 kg/h | >2500 kg/h |
| Reject rate (color sorter) | 1–5 % of feed | >8 % of feed | >12 % of feed |
| Motor amperage (main drive) | 12–18 A | >20 A | >25 A |

## 6. Shutdown Sequence

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Reduce feed rate to minimum (10 % of max) and allow equipment to clear product |
| 2 | Stop feed system |
| 3 | Continue operating screen and color sorter for 2 minutes to clear remaining product |
| 4 | Stop color sorter |
| 5 | Close compressed air supply to color sorter |
| 6 | Stop screening deck |
| 7 | Stop discharge conveyors after all material has cleared |
| 8 | Stop dust collection system |
| 9 | Perform visual inspection of all equipment — note any abnormal findings |
| 10 | Log shutdown in equipment logbook: time, operator name, total run hours, any issues |
| 11 | If changeover to different product, proceed to Section 8: Cleaning Procedure |

## 7. Emergency Shutdown Procedure

### Activation
Press any red Emergency Stop (E-Stop) button or the main E-Stop panel switch. This immediately cuts power to all drives.

### Upon Emergency Shutdown
1. **Do NOT reset** until the cause has been identified and corrected
2. Announce "Emergency shutdown — cleaning and grading line stopped" over the intercom
3. Identify the reason for the ESD:
   - Mechanical jam / blockage
   - Fire or smoke
   - Personnel injury or entrapment
   - Electrical fault / motor overload trip
   - Dust explosion risk (excessive dust cloud)
4. Secure the area — prevent re-entry by unauthorized personnel
5. Correct the cause following the appropriate troubleshooting section (Section 9)
6. Verify all personnel are clear before resetting E-stops
7. Follow full Startup Sequence (Section 4) after an ESD

### Fire or Dust Explosion
- Activate E-stop immediately
- Do NOT open any equipment enclosure — oxygen feeds fire/explosion
- Activate fire suppression system if present
- Evacuate the area
- Notify plant emergency coordinator

## 8. Cleaning Procedure

### 8.1 Between Products (Changeover Cleaning)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Complete full Shutdown Sequence (Section 6) |
| 2 | LOTO the equipment — lock out main power |
| 3 | Open inspection doors on all equipment |
| 4 | Vacuum clean all interior surfaces: screens, magnet housings, color sorter chutes, conveyor belts |
| 5 | Remove and clean magnet assemblies with approved non-abrasive cloth |
| 6 | Wipe down color sorter optics with approved lens cleaner and lint-free wipes |
| 7 | Vacuum dust collector filter surfaces |
| 8 | Inspect all seals and gaskets — replace if damaged |
| 9 | Close all inspection doors |
| 10 | Remove LOTO — proceed to Pre-Start Checks (Section 3) |

### 8.2 Daily Cleaning

- Clean magnet assemblies every 4 hours of operation (log in Magnet Audit Log)
- Sweep or vacuum floor area around equipment
- Empty dust collection bins
- Wipe exterior surfaces with damp cloth
- Clean color sorter viewing windows

### 8.3 Deep Cleaning (Weekly or after 50 operating hours)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Screen decks | Remove, wash with warm water (40–50 °C) and mild detergent (1 % solution, pH 6–8), rinse with clean water, air dry before reinstallation |
| Magnet assemblies | Remove ferrous debris using brass scraper (avoid scratching surface), wipe with isopropyl alcohol, inspect for cracks |
| Color sorter interior | Compressed air blow-down all compartments, clean lenses with optical-grade cleaner and microfiber, inspect LED banks, verify air nozzle alignment |
| Dust collector | Replace filter bags if pressure drop exceeds 150 Pa, clean plenum |

### 8.4 Chemical Cleaning (Monthly / As Needed)

| Chemical | Concentration | Contact Time | Rinse Temp | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-------------|------------|-------|
| Alkaline cleaner (pH 10–11) | 2–3 % v/v | 15 min | 50–60 °C | For oily residues on screens |
| Sanitizer (peracetic acid) | 0.2 % v/v | 5 min | Cold rinse | Food-contact surfaces only |
| Isopropyl alcohol (99 %) | Neat | Wipe & dry | N/A | Electronics / optics only |

## 9. Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Probable Cause | Remedy | Reference |
|---------|---------------|--------|-----------|
| Excessive fines in graded product | Screen deck has holes or tears | Inspect and replace screen panel | Screen swap procedure, Section 8.3 |
| Poor separation on screen deck | Screen blinded or overloaded | Reduce feed rate; clean or replace screen | Section 8.3 deep cleaning |
| Magnet not removing ferrous debris | Magnet surface weakened or debris built up | Clean magnet; measure Gauss; replace if <7000 Gauss | Gauss meter check, Section 5 |
| Color sorter rejecting good product | Optics dirty; calibration drifted | Clean lenses; run auto-calibration cycle | Section 8.3, weekly calibration |
| Color sorter missing contaminants | Compressed air pressure low; nozzle clogged | Check/restore air pressure to 6–8 bar; clean nozzle | Section 5, Section 8.3 |
| High motor amperage on screen drive | Bearing wear; mechanical binding | Inspect bearings; check for foreign material | Shutdown and inspect |
| Dust collector high pressure drop | Filters clogged | Pulse clean or replace filter bags | Section 8.3 |
| Excessive vibration or noise | Loose mountings; unbalanced screen | Tighten bolts; check screen tension | Visual inspection |
| Color sorter not starting | Interlock open; air pressure low | Close cabin door; restore compressed air | Section 3 pre-start |
| Reject rate >12 % | Severe calibration drift or optics failure | Stop line; run full calibration; contact maintenance if persistent | Section 3 / 7 |

## 10. Preventive Maintenance

| Task | Frequency | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Magnet cleaning | Every 4 operating hours | Remove ferrous debris with brass scraper; log in Magnet Audit Log |
| Color sorter calibration | Weekly | Run auto-calibration routine; verify reject accuracy |
| Screen deck swap | Every 500 operating hours | Replace screen panels; inspect frame for wear |
| Color sorter optics cleaning | Weekly | Clean lenses with optical cleaner + microfiber |
| Bearing lubrication (screen drive) | Every 200 operating hours | Grease with NLGI #2 lithium grease |
| Compressed air filter/dryer maintenance | Monthly | Drain and clean; replace filter element if needed |
| Dust collector filter replacement | Every 1000 operating hours or when ΔP >150 Pa | Replace filter bags |
| Belt tension adjustment (conveyors) | Every 300 operating hours | Adjust per manufacturer spec |
| Motor winding insulation test | Annually | Megger test at 500 V; minimum 1 MΩ |
| Color sorter LED bank inspection | Every 6 months | Inspect for failed LEDs; replace bank if >5 % failed |
| Full system overhaul | Annually or 4000 operating hours | Bearings, belts, seals, screen decks, magnet assemblies, full calibration |
