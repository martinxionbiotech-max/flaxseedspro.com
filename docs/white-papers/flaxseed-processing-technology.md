## Executive Summary

Flaxseed processing technology has evolved significantly over the past decade. This white paper examines current best practices in cold pressing, milling stabilization, and protein fractionation.

## Processing Innovations

### Cold Press Optimization
- Temperature-controlled screw pressing below 40°C preserves 98% of ALA content
- Pre-heating seeds to 30°C improves oil yield by 8-10% without quality degradation
- Nitrogen blanketing during pressing reduces peroxide formation by 60%

### Milling Stabilization
- Nitrogen flushing immediately post-milling extends shelf life from 2 to 9 months
- Low-temperature milling (< 45°C product temp) prevents fatty acid oxidation
- Particle size distribution optimization reduces rancidity surface area

### Protein Fractionation
- Dry fractionation yields 40-50% protein content without chemical solvents
- Air classification separates protein from fiber fractions at 95% efficiency
- Functional properties (emulsification, water holding) comparable to soy protein
