# Flaxseed Hull — Product Application Guide

## 1. Physical & Functional Properties

| Property | Value | Method |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Water holding capacity (WHC) | 6–8 g/g | Baumann apparatus / centrifugation |
| Oil holding capacity (OHC) | 2–3 g/g | Centrifugation |
| Swelling capacity | 12–18 mL/g | Bed volume technique |
| SDG lignan content | 7–10 mg/g | HPLC |
| Dietary fibre (total) | 45–55% w/w | AOAC 991.43 |
| Bulk density | 0.35–0.50 g/mL | Loose & tapped |
| Colour | Light to medium tan | Hunter L: 60–75 |
| Particle size (typical) | 80–500 µm | Sieve analysis |

Flaxseed hull is the mechanically decorticated outer layer of brown or golden flaxseed. It is rich in mucilage (soluble fibre), lignans (primarily secoisolariciresinol diglucoside — SDG), and a residual protein–fibre matrix. The high WHC and swelling capacity dominate its behaviour in food systems.

## 2. Application Categories

### 2.1 Bakery Products

| Category | Examples | Usage Level (%) | Functional Benefit | Processing Considerations | Sensory Impact |
|----------|----------|-----------------|-------------------|--------------------------|---------------|
| Bread (pan, hearth) | White, wholemeal, multigrain | 3–8 | WHC improves dough yield and crumb moisture; fibre enrichment; SDG antioxidant extends mould-free shelf life | Increase water by 1.2–1.5× hull weight; extend mixing time by 1–2 min; final proof may need +5 min | Mild nutty/nutty-earthy flavour; hull particles visible at >6%; crumb colour darkens slightly |
| Muffins & quick breads | Blueberry, bran, carrot cake | 5–12 | Moisture retention extends shelf life by 2–3 d; soluble fibre improves batter viscosity | Add hull with dry ingredients; increase total liquid by hull weight × 3–4; reduce leavening by 5–10% to compensate for gas cell disruption | Heartier mouthfeel; can mask fruit notes if >10%; hull flecks visible |
| Cookies | Shortbread, oatmeal, digestive | 5–15 | WHC controls spread; fibre reduces fat absorption during bake | Reduce flour by hull weight; no water adjustment needed in short-dough systems; chill dough 15 min to limit spread | Grainy texture above 10%; flavour complements oat/nut profiles; slightly darker bake |
| Tortillas | Corn, wheat, whole-grain | 4–10 | Improves rollability and pliability; reduces brittleness on day 2–3 | Hydrate hull with 4× water for 10 min before adding to masa/dough; steam time +10% | Minimal flavour at ≤6%; noticeable nuttiness at 8–10%; slight tan colour |

### 2.2 Cereals & Bars

| Category | Usage Level (%) | Benefit | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| Breakfast cereal (flakes, clusters) | 5–15 | WHC adds bowl-life stability; fibre boosts satiety | Co-mill with base grain; avoid >10% in puffed cereals (texture issues) |
| Granola & muesli | 5–12 | Nutty note complements oats; SDG antioxidant stabilises nuts/oils | Toast hull lightly before addition for flavour; no water adjustment needed |
| Nutrition/meal-replacement bars | 5–10 | Binds moisture, reduces water activity (aw); extends softness | Use fine-milled hull (≤200 µm) to avoid grittiness; add with dry binder; adjust humectant (glycerol) down by 20–30% |
| Protein/energy bars | 3–8 | WHC reduces syneresis; fibre masks whey-protein chalkiness | Co-process hull with protein powder; water adjustment needed: +2 mL/g hull |

### 2.3 Meat Analogues

| Category | Usage Level (%) | Benefit | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| Plant-based burgers | 3–8 | WHC improves juiciness; fibre strengthens texturised structure | Pre-hydrate hull (1:5 hull:water); add during chopping/emulsification; reduce added water by hull water uptake |
| Sausages & meatballs | 4–10 | OHC binds fat; reduces cook loss by 15–25% | Add as dry powder; hold mix 10 min before stuffing; adjust salt up slightly |
| Nuggets & strips | 3–6 | Improves coating adhesion; fibre boosts nutritional profile | Blend hull with breading/flour mix at 10–20% of coating weight |

### 2.4 Dairy Alternatives

| Category | Usage Level (%) | Benefit | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|---------|-------|

## 3. Formulation Guidelines

### 3.1 Water Adjustment for High-WHC Products

The high WHC (6–8 g/g) of flaxseed hull means it competes for free water in formulations.

**Rule of thumb:**
- For every 1 g of hull added, increase total formula water by 3–5 g.
- In doughs: add water as 50% pre-hydration (stand 10 min) + 50% in final mix.
- For batters: pre-blend hull with a portion of total water (1:8 hull:water) 5 min before adding other dry ingredients.

**Bakers' percentage formula adjustment (bread example):**
- Base formula: 100% flour, 60% water
- With 5% hull (flour basis): increase water to 75–80%, reduce flour by 5%

### 3.2 Flavour Masking for Bitter Notes

Flaxseed hull may contribute mild bitterness or astringency from phenolic compounds (lignans, phenolic acids).

| Strategy | Application | Dosage |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Sweetener synergy | Bakery, bars | Sucrose + 2% invert syrup masks bitterness |
| Acidic counterbalance | Dressings, sauces | Citric acid 0.1–0.3% or vinegar |
| Flavour masking agents | Powders, supplements | Vanillin 0.05–0.1%, cocoa powder 2–5%, or natural butter flavour |
| Encapsulation | Beverages, dairy alt. | Blending hull lipophilic fraction with maltodextrin (DE 10–15) |
| Roasting | All categories | Dry-roast hull at 140 °C for 8–12 min to develop nutty notes & suppress bitter phenolics |
| Co-processing with legumes | Meat analogues | Chickpea or fava flour at 2:1 ratio attenuates bitterness |

### 3.3 Particle Size Selection

| Mesh Size | µm Range | Best For |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| 40–60 mesh | 250–420 | Tortillas, cookies (visible fleck desired), pet food |
| 60–80 mesh | 177–250 | Bread, cereals, granola, meat analogues |
| 80–100 mesh | 149–177 | Muffins, bars, dairy alternatives, yoghurts |
| <100 mesh (micronised) | <149 | Powders, beverages, supplements, smooth sauces |
| 200 mesh (micro-milled) | <74 | Capsules, tablets, infant nutrition, clear beverages (colloidal suspension) |

## 4. Label Claims

### 4.1 Fibre Content Claims

| Claim Type | EU (Reg. 1924/2006) | US (FDA 21 CFR 101.54) | China (GB 28050-2011) |
|------------|----------------------|------------------------|-----------------------|
| **Source of fibre** | ≥3 g fibre/100 g or ≥1.5 g/100 kcal | ≥2.5 g fibre/RACC (serving) | ≥3 g fibre/100 g |
| **Good source of fibre** | — *(not used; "source" is the base level)* | ≥3.5 g fibre/serving (rounded to nearest whole gram) | — *(China uses "source" only)* |
| **High / rich in fibre** | ≥6 g fibre/100 g or ≥3 g/100 kcal | ≥5 g fibre/serving | ≥6 g fibre/100 g |

**Typical fibre content of flax hull:** 45–55 g/100 g.
- At 5% inclusion: ~2.5 g fibre/100 g product → qualifies as "source" in EU (if RACC ≥100 g).
- At 10% inclusion: ~5 g fibre/100 g → qualifies as "high" in EU and "good source" in US.

### 4.2 SDG Lignan Claims

SDG content in flax hull: **7–10 mg/g** (7,000–10,000 ppm).

- A 10 g serving of hull delivers 70–100 mg SDG.
- No authorised health claim exists specifically for SDG in most markets; structure–function claims may be possible in the US (e.g., "SDG is a lignan phytoestrogen that provides antioxidant support").
- EU: Not permitted to make health claims under Reg. 1924/2006 unless authorised via EFSA.

### 4.3 Example Claim Statements

- **EU:** "Flaxseed hull is a source of fibre. Consuming a 30 g portion contributes 7.5 g fibre."
- **US:** "Excellent source of fibre (9 g per serving). Made with flaxseed hull."
- **China:** "含膳食纤维 (Contains dietary fibre). 每100克含6克膳食纤维 (Contains 6 g fibre per 100 g)."

## 5. Regulatory Status

| Market | Status | Details |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **European Union** | Novel Food (Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2020/1288) | Defatted flaxseed hull approved as novel food under Regulation (EU) 2015/2283. Authorised for use in bread, bakery wares, breakfast cereals, muesli, fruit/vegetable blends, yoghurt, beverages (excluding fruit juice), soups, salads and meal replacements. Max levels specified per category (typically 3–15%). |
| **United States** | GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe) | Flaxseed hull (including defatted) has GRAS status via self-affirmed GRAS notifications. Widely accepted as food ingredient. |
| **China** | Traditional food ingredient | Flaxseed has a long history of food use in China, particularly in northern regions (Inner Mongolia, Gansu, Xinjiang). Hull is considered a conventional food ingredient; no novel food notification required. |
| **Canada** | Accepted as food ingredient | Flaxseed hull is an accepted food ingredient; Health Canada has issued no objection letters for its use. |
| **Japan** | Conventional food | Flaxseed and its fractions are used in health foods; no pre-market approval needed. |
| **Australia/NZ** | Novel food (per component) | Whole flaxseed is traditional food; isolated hull fractions may require FSANZ novel food assessment on a case-by-case basis. |

## 6. Storage Stability in Finished Products

| Product Type | Expected Shelf Life | Key Stability Factors | Accelerating Factors |
|--------------|---------------------|-----------------------|---------------------|
| Dry bakery (bread, tortillas, cookies) | Bread: 5–7 d (ambient); Cookies: 3–6 mo | WHC retains moisture at low aw; SDG radical-scavenging extends oxidative stability; hull mucilage retards starch retrogradation by ~20% | High humidity (>65% RH) accelerates staling in bread; light degrades SDG by ~10–15% over 6 mo |
| Cereals & bars | 9–12 mo | Fibre acts as binder and aw control; SDG protects unsaturated fats | Temperature >30 °C accelerates lipid oxidation; hull moisture >8% promotes mould |
| Meat analogues | 6–9 mo (frozen); 7–10 d (chilled) | OHC retains fat; WHC reduces drip loss; SDG delays rancidity in plant oils | Freeze–thaw cycles degrade texture; O₂ ingress accelerates oxidation |
| Dairy alternatives | 7–14 d (chilled); 6–9 mo (UHT/aseptic) | Soluble fibre stabilises emulsion; SDG antioxidant protects ALA in added oils | Light exposure (especially UV) degrades lignans; >40 °C accelerates sedimentation |
| Nutritional powders | 18–24 mo | Low aw (<0.3) ensures microbial stability; SDG stable in dry state | High humidity (>50% RH) causes caking; heat >50 °C degrades lignan bioactivity |
| Pet food (kibble) | 12–18 mo | Fibre maintains texture; antioxidants extend fat stability | Fat oxidation primary concern; high moisture (>10%) causes mould |

**Packaging recommendations:**
- Dry products: OPP/PE or metallised film; N₂ flushing for extended shelf life.
- Chilled products: MAP (70% N₂ / 30% CO₂) or vacuum skin pack.
- Powders: Foil laminate with oxygen scavenger sachet.
- Light-sensitive applications: Use opaque packaging or UV-blocking film.

## 7. Additional Technical Notes

- **Synergy with hydrocolloids:** Hull mucilage (soluble fibre fraction) synergises with xanthan gum (viscosity synergy ~1.2×) and guar gum. Use at 2:1 hull to gum ratio in sauces and dressings.
- **Enzymatic treatment:** Cellulase/hemicellulase (0.1–0.5% w/w) can reduce viscosity in high-hull liquid systems; use at 45–55 °C for 30 min.
- **Emulsion stability:** Hull particles (particularly fat-bound fraction) can act as Pickering stabilisers at 3–5% in oil-in-water emulsions.
- **Allergen declaration:** Flaxseed is not a major allergen in most jurisdictions (not in US Big 9 or EU Annex II list), but isolated cases of allergy exist. Best practice: label advisory "May contain flaxseed" if processed on shared lines.
