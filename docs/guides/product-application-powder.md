# Flaxseed Powder (Milled Flaxseed) — Product Application Guide

## 1. Physical & Functional Properties

Flaxseed powder (also called milled flaxseed or flax meal) is produced by grinding whole or partially defatted flaxseed. It retains the full nutritional matrix — fibre, protein, oil (ALA), lignans, and micronutrients — in a free-flowing powder form.

| Property | Whole Flaxseed Powder | Defatted Flaxseed Powder |
|----------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Fat content | 38–44% | 8–15% |
| Protein | 18–24% | 30–40% |
| Total dietary fibre | 25–35% | 40–50% |
| WHC | 4–6 g/g | 5–7 g/g |
| OHC | 1.5–2.5 g/g | 1–2 g/g |
| ALA content | 20–25% | 4–8% |
| SDG lignans | 3–5 mg/g | 6–9 mg/g |
| Bulk density | 0.45–0.60 g/mL | 0.40–0.55 g/mL |
| Water activity (aw) | 0.35–0.45 | 0.25–0.35 |
| Colour | Light tan to golden | Light cream to beige |
| Particle size (D90) | 200–500 µm | 100–300 µm |

**Key note:** Whole flaxseed powder provides the full ALA omega-3 content but requires careful oxidation management. Defatted flaxseed powder has superior shelf stability and higher fibre concentration, making it suitable for extended shelf-life applications.

## 2. Application Categories

### 2.1 Bakery Products

| Product | Usage Level (%) | Benefit | Processing Considerations | Sensory Impact |
|---------|-----------------|---------|--------------------------|----------------|
| Bread (pan, artisan) | 5–15 | WHC improves dough yield; ALA enriches omega-3; SDG provides antioxidant; improves crumb softness | Increase water by 2–3× powder weight; extend mixing by 1–2 min; reduce yeast by 5–10% (fibre slows gas diffusion); proof time +5–10 min | Mild nutty/earthy flavour at ≤10%; visible specks; darker crust; denser crumb at >12% |
| Muffins & cake | 10–25 | Moisture retention extends shelf life (2–3 d longer); WHC controls batter viscosity | Add to dry blend; increase liquid by 2.5–3× powder weight; reduce fat by 10–15% (powder contributes oil); increase leavening by 5% if dense | Nutty flavour complements whole grains; moist crumb; darker (golden-brown); specks visible |
| Cookies | 15–30 | Reduces spread (WHC binds water); fibre enriches; ALA content | Reduce flour by powder weight; may need +2–5% water if spread too limited; bake at 170–175 °C (vs 180 °C) | Dense, chewy texture; nutty/buttery flavour; dark golden colour |
| Crackers & crispbreads | 10–20 | Fibre enrichment; crisp texture from flax gel | Laminate dough; bake at 180–190 °C for shorter time; hull mucilage can improve lamination | Pronounced nutty/earthy flavour; dark specks; crunchy texture |
| Gluten-free bakery | 10–25 | WHC replaces gluten's water-binding; ALA improves nutritional profile; gel formation aids structure | Blend with rice flour (1:2 or 1:3) + xanthan gum (0.5–1%); increase water by 3× powder weight; longer mix time | Dense but moist; less elastic than gluten; nutty flavour enhancement |
| Tortillas (wheat/corn) | 5–15 | Improves pliability; WHC maintains moisture; reduces cracking after cooling | Pre-hydrate powder (1:4 powder:water) for 10 min; reduce water by hydration volume; hot-press at 190–200 °C | Slight nutty note; tan colour; flexible texture retained 3–5 d |

## 3. Formulation Guidelines

### 3.1 Water Adjustment

Flaxseed powder WHC is 4–6 g/g (whole) or 5–7 g/g (defatted), requiring careful water management.

| Powder Type | Water Absorption Ratio | Recommended Water Adjustment |
|-------------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| Whole flax powder | 1:4 to 1:5 | Add 4–5 mL water per g of powder |
| Defatted flax powder | 1:5 to 1:6 | Add 5–6 mL water per g of powder |
| Micro-milled (<100 µm) | 1:5 to 1:7 | Add 5–7 mL water per g of powder (higher surface area) |

**Practical steps:**
1. Pre-hydrate powder with a portion of formula water (1:5 ratio, 10 min rest) before adding to main mix.
2. In baked goods, reduce initial dough water by 30%, then add the remaining as needed during mixing.
3. For cold beverages, pre-blend with liquid in a high-shear blender (≥5,000 rpm, 30 s).

### 3.2 Flavour Masking

Flaxseed powder has a characteristic nutty/earthy flavour that may need masking in delicate applications.

| Application | Masking Strategy | Dosage |
|-------------|------------------|--------|
| Beverages | Vanilla extract + stevia/enzymatically modified stevia | Vanilla 0.1–0.2%; sweetener to 6–8° Brix |
| Savoury sauces | Roasted onion/garlic powder, yeast extract, smoked paprika | 0.5–2% of formula |
| Protein shakes | Chocolate (5–8%), peanut butter (3–5%), cinnamon (0.2–0.5%) | Cocoa masks earthy notes best |
| Yoghurt / dairy alt. | Fruit purée (5–10%), honey (3–5%), lemon zest (0.1–0.3%) | Berry/citrus masks well |
| Bakery | Cinnamon, nutmeg, brown sugar, molasses | Warm spices at 0.3–1% complement nutty notes |
| General (all) | Vanillin (0.05–0.1%), ethyl maltol (20–50 ppm), salt (0.2–0.5%) | Broad-spectrum masking |

### 3.3 Particle Size Selection

| Grind (mesh) | Particle Size (µm) | Best For | Notes |
|-------------|--------------------|----------|-------|
| Coarse (20–40 mesh) | 400–850 | Cereals, granola, bread (visible texture desired) | Rapid hydration; gritty in smooth applications |
| Medium (40–60 mesh) | 250–400 | Cookies, muffins, tortillas, crackers | Balanced hydration; mild textural presence |
| Fine (60–80 mesh) | 180–250 | Bread (smooth), sauces, patties | Good hydration; minimal grit |
| Extra-fine (80–100 mesh) | 150–180 | Yoghurt, ice cream, dressings | Smooth mouthfeel; disperses well |
| Micronised (<100 mesh) | <150 | Beverages, protein shakes, smooth sauces | Near-invisible; best for clear/texture-sensitive products |
| Micro-milled (200 mesh) | <74 | Infant foods, nutritional powders, fine emulsions | Colloidal stability; best dispersibility |

## 6. Storage Stability

| Form | Shelf Life | Conditions | Critical Factors |
|------|------------|------------|------------------|
| Whole flax powder (ambient) | 3–6 months | <25 °C, <50% RH, opaque sealed packaging | Lipid oxidation (ALA); hexanal >1 ppm = ranci d |
| Whole flax powder (refrigerated) | 6–9 months | <10 °C, sealed bag | Slows oxidation by ~50% |
| Whole flax powder (frozen) | 12–18 months | −18 °C, sealed | Excellent stability |
| Defatted flax powder (ambient) | 12–18 months | <25 °C, <50% RH, sealed | Lower fat = much slower oxidation |
| Micro-milled powder | 6–9 months (ambient) | <20 °C, O₂ barrier packaging | Higher surface area accelerates oxidation |

**Packaging:** Foil laminate with O₂ scavenger recommended for whole powder. N₂ flushing extends shelf life by 40–60%.

## 7. Additional Technical Notes

- **Mucilage extraction:** Flax powder soaked in water (1:10) produces a viscous gel. Pre-extracted mucilage can be dried and used as a clean-label thickener in sauces.
- **Enzyme treatment:** Cellulase (0.2% w/w, 45 °C, 30 min) reduces viscosity by 40% in high-powder liquid products.
- **Cold solubility:** Fine-milled defatted powder disperses in cold water without lumping when pre-mixed with sugar or maltodextrin (1:1 ratio).
- **Egg replacement:** 1 tbsp flax powder + 3 tbsp water = 1 egg in baking. Hydrate 5 min before use. Best for cookies, muffins, and pancakes.
- **Fat replacement:** Flax powder can replace 25–50% of shortening in bakery with added water (1:3 powder:water slurry) to maintain dough consistency.
