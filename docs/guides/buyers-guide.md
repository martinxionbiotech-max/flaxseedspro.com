# Buyer's Guide

---

## How to Choose a Flaxseed Supplier

### 1. Quality Specifications

Start with the analytical specs that matter for your application:

- **ALA Omega-3 Content**: 52-57% is industry standard; verify with COA
- **Purity**: Look for 99.9% minimum; lower purity means more cleaning waste
- **Moisture**: ≤ 8% ensures stability; higher moisture risks mold
- **Microbiological**: Confirm pathogen testing (Salmonella, E. coli negative)

### 2. Certifications

| Certification | Why It Matters |
|:--------------|:---------------|
| HACCP | Foundational food safety system |
| ISO 22000 | Internationally recognized FSMS |
| FSSC 22000 | GFSI-benchmarked scheme |
| Organic | Required for organic-labeled products |
| Non-GMO | Required for non-GMO claims |
| Kosher | Required for kosher-certified products |
| Gluten-Free | Required for gluten-free claims |

### 3. Supplier Evaluation Checklist

- [ ] Facility audit conducted within last 12 months
- [ ] COA provided with every shipment
- [ ] Batch traceability from seed to shipment
- [ ] Third-party lab testing on record
- [ ] Allergen control program in place
- [ ] Recall and contingency plan documented
- [ ] Consistent on-time delivery history

### 4. Sampling Protocol

When evaluating suppliers, request:
- A representative sample (minimum 1 kg of whole seed or meal)
- Current COA (within 30 days)
- Product specification sheet
- Certifications (current certificates)
- Sample retention for your own independent testing

### 5. Red Flags

- Unable or unwilling to provide COA for current inventory
- No third-party audits or certifications
- Cannot identify growing region or variety
- Inconsistent analytical results across samples
- Poor communication or slow response time
